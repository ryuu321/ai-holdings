ChatGPT Prompts for Nutritionists & Dietitians

15 free prompts to communicate better with clients, grow your practice, and save hours on administrative writing every week.

Registered dietitians and nutritionists spend more time than they should on emails, handouts, social posts, and session notes — writing that doesn't require your clinical expertise. These 15 prompts handle the words so your expertise can focus where it matters: your clients.

Important: These prompts assist with communication, education, and practice management — not with clinical assessment or individualized medical nutrition therapy. Never input client names, diagnoses, lab values, or any protected health information (PHI) into AI tools. All AI-generated nutrition content must be reviewed by a qualified professional for clinical accuracy before use. Content intended for clients should not substitute for individualized dietary advice.

1. Client Communication

Prompt 1 — Initial Consultation Welcome

Write a pre-consultation email to send to a new nutrition client. My practice: [private practice / clinic / telehealth / corporate wellness] My specialty: [weight management / sports nutrition / eating disorders / pediatric / diabetes / GI health / general wellness] What I want them to know before their first session: [what to expect / how to prepare / what we'll cover] Forms to complete beforehand: [food diary / health history / intake questionnaire] Write: Subject line → Warm opening → What to expect in their first session (not overwhelming — just clear) → How to prepare (food diary tips, medications list, health history) → What success looks like in our work together (hope without promises) → Easy next step. Under 300 words. Warm and encouraging, not clinical.
Pro tip: Most new clients arrive with years of diet culture baggage. A pre-consultation email that signals a judgment-free approach will dramatically reduce no-shows.

Prompt 2 — Between-Session Check-In

Write between-session check-in templates for nutrition clients. My approach: [goal-focused / intuitive eating / medical nutrition therapy / habit-based coaching] Check-in frequency: [weekly / biweekly / as needed] What I want to track: [how they're feeling / specific behavior changes / energy levels / challenges] Write 3 check-in formats: (1) Email check-in (6 open questions — not about the scale, about how they're living and feeling), (2) SMS check-in (2–3 questions, conversational), (3) Google Form questions (10 items covering habits, energy, challenges, wins, questions for next session). All formats: non-judgmental, curiosity-focused, not compliance-police tone.

Prompt 3 — Difficult Behavior Change Conversation

Help me prepare for a difficult client conversation about behavior change. Situation: [client not following plan / client fixated on the scale / client comparing to previous dietitian / client questioning evidence-based approach / client in a plateau / client avoiding certain foods out of fear] Write: (1) How to open the conversation (empathy first, not problem-solving first), (2) 3 questions to understand their perspective, (3) How to redirect without dismissing their concern, (4) How to collaboratively adjust the plan rather than lecturing, (5) What NOT to say (common phrases that backfire). Client relationships are built or broken in these moments — prepare for them.

2. Practice Marketing

Prompt 4 — Instagram Content Plan

Build a 30-day Instagram content plan for my nutrition practice. My specialty: [describe] My target audience: [describe — busy moms / athletes / people with diabetes / disordered eating recovery / general wellness seekers] My content tone: [evidence-based and approachable / anti-diet / food freedom / clinical but warm] Compliance note: No before/after claims, no weight loss guarantees, no medical claims. 30 content ideas: For each — format (reel / carousel / static / stories poll), topic, hook sentence, and key points to cover. Content mix: nutrition myth-busting → practical meal prep tips → behind-the-scenes (a day in your practice) → client questions answered → emotional relationship with food → quick recipe ideas. 80% education, 20% practice-related.

Prompt 5 — Referral Outreach to Healthcare Providers

Write outreach emails to build referral relationships with healthcare providers. My specialty: [describe — what conditions I work with, what I don't] Providers I want to connect with: [PCPs / gastroenterologists / endocrinologists / OB-GYNs / psychiatrists / bariatric surgeons / sports medicine physicians] What I offer their patients: [evidence-based nutrition counseling — describe your approach and credentials] Write: (1) Introduction email to a primary care physician (150 words — specific to patient populations they see, clear on what I do and don't do), (2) Email to a gastroenterologist (GI-focused angle), (3) Follow-up email after no response (1 week — shorter, different angle). Goal: referral relationship, not a sales pitch.
Pro tip: Physicians refer to dietitians they trust will reinforce the physician's recommendations. Emphasize collaboration, not competition.

Prompt 6 — Workshop or Webinar Promotion

Write promotional content for a nutrition workshop or webinar. Event title and topic: [e.g., "Eating for Energy: Why You're Always Tired and What to Do About It" / "Meal Prep in 2 Hours: A Realistic Guide for Busy People"] Format: [in-person / virtual / hybrid] Date and time: [placeholder] Who it is for: [target audience] What they will learn: [3–5 takeaways] Price or free: [free / $X] Write: (1) Event landing page copy (title → subheader → who this is for → what you'll learn (bullets) → about the presenter → registration CTA), (2) 3 social media promotional posts (different hooks each), (3) Email to existing clients/subscribers announcing the event. No medical outcome promises.

3. Client Education

Prompt 7 — Nutrition Concept Explainer

Write plain-language explainers for common nutrition concepts my clients ask about. Topics: [blood sugar balance / protein at every meal / dietary fiber / reading nutrition labels / hydration basics / anti-inflammatory eating / gut microbiome basics / intuitive eating / hunger vs. appetite / macronutrients — choose 3] For each: (1) What is it, in one sentence a 12-year-old would understand, (2) Why it matters to their daily life (not abstract science), (3) One practical thing they can do this week, (4) Common misconception to clear up, (5) When it gets more complicated (and they should ask me directly). Each under 250 words. No specific clinical recommendations — these are educational, not personalized advice.

Prompt 8 — Meal Planning Education Handout

Write a meal planning education handout for clients who are new to structured eating. My client type: [busy professional / family with kids / athlete / chronic condition management / general wellness] Time they have to cook: [15 minutes / 30 minutes / batch cooking on weekends] Skill level: [beginner — rarely cooks / intermediate / comfortable in the kitchen] Dietary pattern: [balanced / plant-forward / lower carb / Mediterranean / no restriction — just balanced] Create: (1) "The 3-2-1 Method" for weekly meal prep (or invent a simple framework I can name), (2) 5 sample meal building formulas (protein + veggie + starch + fat), (3) A pantry staples list (20 items that make any meal possible), (4) A simple one-week structure (not a strict meal plan — just a framework), (5) How to make it work when life gets busy. Print-friendly format.

4. Content Creation

Prompt 9 — Blog Post for SEO

Write a blog post outline for my nutrition practice website. Topic options: [5 signs you are not eating enough protein / the truth about "clean eating" / how to stop stress eating / what to eat before and after a workout / meal prep for beginners / why diets fail and what works instead] Chosen topic: [paste] Target reader: [describe your typical client] SEO goal: [rank locally / attract new clients / build authority] Outline: H1 (SEO-optimized, not clickbait) → Meta description (155 chars) → Hook intro (start with their struggle, not the solution) → 5–7 H2 sections with key points → FAQ section (4 questions with brief answers) → Conclusion with CTA to book a session. Include primary keyword and 3 natural secondary keywords to use throughout.

Prompt 10 — Email Newsletter

Write a monthly email newsletter for my nutrition practice subscribers. Month / season: [describe — January reset / summer / back-to-school / holiday season] Main topic: [one theme for the month — energy / gut health / hydration / emotional eating / family nutrition] My practice style: [warm and practical / evidence-based / anti-diet / holistic] Newsletter sections: (1) "From my kitchen / practice" — personal note (100 words, relatable, not clinical), (2) Nutrition tip of the month — one actionable idea, (3) Recipe or meal idea — simple, practical, no exotic ingredients, (4) "Ask me anything" — answer one common client question (general, not personalized advice), (5) Practice news + booking reminder. Conversational, not academic.

5. Practice Management

Prompt 11 — Session Note Framework

Help me build a structured session note template to speed up my documentation. Session types I need templates for: [initial assessment / follow-up / final session / corporate group session] Information I always need to capture: [progress on goals / barriers / adjustments to plan / client questions / next session focus] My documentation system: [paper / EHR / Google Docs / Notion] Create: Draft SOAP-style (or your preferred format) note template for each session type — with prompts to fill in, not full sentences (I'll add clinical detail). The template should take under 5 minutes to complete. Include: space for client language (their words, not clinical translation) — this is often the most useful detail to capture. Note: Final clinical documentation must meet your jurisdiction's standards and be completed by you, not AI.
Pro tip: Capturing exact client language in session notes ("I just feel like I fail every Sunday") gives you material for deeper conversations in future sessions.

Prompt 12 — Package and Pricing Page Copy

Write the copy for my nutrition services and pricing page. Services I offer: [initial consultation / follow-up sessions / packages / group programs / corporate wellness / online courses] Pricing: [insert your actual pricing — or mark as TBD] What makes me different: [my credentials / approach / specialization / results clients experience] Target client: [describe who this page is written for] Write: (1) Services page intro (under 150 words — speak to their pain, not your credentials), (2) Service descriptions (for each offering: what it is, who it is for, what they get, price), (3) FAQ section (6 questions: insurance / how many sessions / what happens if I cancel / do you work with medical conditions / telehealth / what results to expect), (4) CTA section (no pressure, just clear next step).

6. Client Coaching

Prompt 13 — Goal-Setting Session Framework

Help me structure a goal-setting session with a new nutrition client. My approach: [SMART goals / values-based goals / habit stacking / motivational interviewing] Client type: [general wellness / medical condition / disordered eating recovery / athlete] Common goals clients come in with: [lose weight / have more energy / stop bingeing / run a 5K / manage diabetes / feed my family better] Create: (1) 10 questions to help clients clarify what they really want (not just what they think they should want), (2) Framework for setting 1 behavior goal for the next 2 weeks (not an outcome goal), (3) How to handle "I just want to lose weight" — redirect to behavior without dismissing, (4) A simple one-page goal-tracking sheet they can use between sessions.

7. Professional Development

Prompt 14 — Supervision / Peer Consultation Prep

Help me prepare for clinical supervision or peer consultation. Case I want to discuss (no client names or identifying information): [general situation — e.g., "client with long history of restriction who is now bingeing on weekends and feeling shame"] What I have tried so far: [approaches, interventions] Where I feel stuck: [what is not working / what I am uncertain about] What I want from supervision: [a different perspective / specific technique / validation / ethical guidance] Structure my presentation: (1) Brief case summary (2–3 sentences, no PHI), (2) What I have tried and the outcome, (3) My hypothesis about what is happening, (4) Specific questions I want to explore. Clear supervision prep leads to more useful guidance.

Prompt 15 — Annual Practice Review

Help me review my nutrition practice this year. Clients seen: [number] | Average sessions per client: [number] | Retention rate: [%] Revenue: [$] | Top referral source: [describe] Specialization I worked in most: [describe] What worked well: [list 3] What was hard: [list 3] Goal for next year: [specific] Analyze: (1) My client retention — what patterns explain who stays vs. drops off, (2) My referral pipeline — where did my best clients come from, and how to get more of them, (3) The gap between where I am and my goal — what would need to change, (4) The specialty or population I am best at — should I niche down, (5) The honest question: what am I avoiding that would most grow my practice?
