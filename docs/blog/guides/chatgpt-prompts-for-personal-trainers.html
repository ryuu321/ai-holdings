ChatGPT Prompts for Personal Trainers

15 free prompts to design better programs, keep clients coming back, build your PT business, and create income beyond the gym floor.

Personal training is a people business — but it's also a business business. The trainers who build sustainable $5K–$10K/month careers spend as much time on their systems, communication, and marketing as they do on programming. These 15 prompts help you do both, faster.

Important: These prompts are for business and communication support. Always apply your professional judgment, certifications, and knowledge of each client's health history when designing training programs. AI-generated programs must be reviewed and adapted by a qualified trainer.

1. Program Design

Prompt 1 — Client Training Program Template

Help me design a 4-week training program for a client with the following profile: Age: [age] | Sex: [M/F] | Goal: [fat loss / muscle gain / general fitness / sport-specific / rehab/recovery] Experience level: [beginner / intermediate / advanced] Training days/week: [number] | Session length: [minutes] Equipment available: [gym / home / bodyweight only / list equipment] Injuries or limitations: [list or "none"] Current fitness level: [describe] Create a 4-week program: Week 1 (foundation), Week 2 (progression), Week 3 (intensity), Week 4 (deload or peak). For each workout: exercise names, sets, reps, rest periods, and coaching cues for the key movements. Include warm-up and cooldown templates. Note: I will review and adapt this before using with any client.
Pro tip: Ask AI to generate the structure and exercise selection, then apply your expertise on load, tempo, and the specific client's movement patterns. Never skip your own review.

Prompt 2 — Exercise Progression Library

Build a progression library for [movement pattern: squat / hinge / push / pull / carry / core stability]. For each, list 6 exercises arranged from easiest to most challenging: (1) Beginner/rehabilitative, (2) Beginner/standard, (3) Intermediate, (4) Advanced, (5) Complex/loaded, (6) Sport-specific or elite. Include: coaching cues for each, common mistakes, and which clients each is best suited for. I want this as a reference to select the right progression for each client.

2. Client Communication & Retention

Prompt 3 — Client Onboarding Experience

Design a client onboarding experience for new personal training clients. My training style: [describe your approach] Session format: [in-person gym / online / hybrid] Typical client profile: [who you work with best] Create: (1) Welcome message for a new client (send after booking), (2) Pre-session intake questions (10 questions covering goals, history, lifestyle), (3) What to cover in Session 1 to build trust and set expectations, (4) 7-day check-in message template, (5) 30-day progress conversation guide. The goal: clients who feel seen, supported, and committed to a long engagement.
Pro tip: The onboarding experience determines retention more than the training itself. Clients who feel understood in week 1 stay for months.

Prompt 4 — Client Re-Engagement Message

Write a re-engagement message for a client who has [gone quiet / missed sessions / cancelled their last 2 bookings / stopped responding]. Context: [how long they've been a client, what they were working toward, what might have happened] My goal: [understand what's going on / offer to adjust / save the relationship] Tone: [warm and non-judgmental — not salesy or guilt-tripping] Write 2 versions: (1) Text message (under 100 words), (2) Email (200 words max). Both should open with genuine care, leave the door open, and offer a low-pressure next step.

Prompt 5 — Client Progress Check-In System

Build a weekly check-in system for my [online / in-person hybrid] training clients. Client count: [number I manage] My goal: catch issues early, celebrate wins, adjust programs, maintain accountability Create: (1) A weekly check-in form (10 questions — covering training, nutrition, sleep, energy, stress, wins, challenges), (2) How to review and respond to check-ins efficiently, (3) Red flags in check-in responses that signal a client is about to quit, (4) Response templates for: great week, struggling week, plateau, and breakthrough. Time budget: 5 minutes per client per week.

3. Nutrition Guidance

Prompt 6 — Nutrition Education Content

I want to educate my clients on [nutrition topic: protein intake / calorie deficit / meal timing / hydration / pre/post-workout nutrition / managing cravings / reading food labels]. I am a certified personal trainer — not a registered dietitian. Write a client-friendly educational piece (300–400 words) that: Explains the concept simply → Gives practical, actionable takeaways → Includes common mistakes → Gives a "this week try this" action step. Add a disclaimer that I encourage clients to work with a registered dietitian for personalized nutrition plans.
Pro tip: Always clarify your scope of practice when providing nutrition guidance. Keep it behavioral (meal structure, portion awareness) rather than clinical (specific medical nutrition therapy).

Prompt 7 — Meal Prep Guidance Template

My client wants to improve their nutrition but struggles with meal prep. Their situation: [describe lifestyle — busy parent, works long hours, limited cooking skills, etc.]. Their goal: [fat loss / muscle building / energy / performance]. Dietary preferences/restrictions: [list or "none"]. Write a beginner-friendly meal prep guide: (1) Why meal prep works for their specific goal, (2) The "minimum viable prep" approach — what to batch cook that gives 80% of the benefit, (3) A sample weekly prep routine (Sunday + midweek), (4) 3 simple meal templates they can repeat without getting bored, (5) How to adjust portions based on their hunger and energy levels. Note: This is general guidance — not a personalized meal plan.

4. Marketing & Client Acquisition

Prompt 8 — Social Media Content Strategy

Build a 30-day social media content plan for a personal trainer. My niche: [who I specialize in: busy moms / men over 40 / beginners / athletes / weight loss / muscle building / online clients] Platforms: [Instagram / TikTok / Facebook / LinkedIn] Posting frequency: [X times/week] Content I can create: [video / photos / text / before-after / tips] Create 30 post ideas with: Post type → Topic → Hook (first line or caption opener) → CTA. Mix: educational tips, client transformations (with permission), behind-the-scenes, personal story, myth-busting, and promotional posts. Ratio: ~80% value, ~20% promotional.
Pro tip: Your niche is your marketing power. "Personal trainer for women over 50 who hate the gym" will out-convert "personal trainer" every time.

Prompt 9 — Lead Magnet Creation

Help me create a lead magnet to attract new personal training clients online. My niche: [describe ideal client] Platform I'll promote it on: [Instagram / Facebook / email / website] My goal: [email list growth / DM inquiries / consultation bookings] Suggest 3 lead magnet ideas with: Title, format (PDF / video / quiz / challenge), what's inside, and why it would appeal to my niche. Then write the full copy for my top choice: headline, sub-headline, 5 bullet points of what they'll learn/get, and the opt-in CTA.

Prompt 10 — Discovery Call Sales Script

Write a discovery call script for converting interested prospects into personal training clients. Call length: [20–30 minutes] My service: [in-person / online / hybrid] personal training Starting price: $[amount] / [package description] Common objections I hear: [cost / time / "I'll start after X" / unsure if I can commit] Script structure: (1) Rapport building (2–3 questions), (2) Understanding their goal deeply (4–5 questions), (3) Pain point amplification — why they haven't solved it yet, (4) Presenting my solution, (5) Handling each common objection with a response, (6) Asking for the sale without being pushy, (7) Close or next step. Include transition phrases between each stage.

5. Business Operations & Growth

Prompt 11 — Pricing & Package Strategy

Help me design my training packages and pricing. Current situation: [how many clients, current rates, session frequency] Market: [location or online — what's the going rate in your area] My specialization: [niche, credentials, experience level] Income goal: $[amount]/month Hours I want to train: [maximum billable hours/week] Design: (1) 3-tier package structure (starter / standard / premium), (2) Pricing for each with justification, (3) How to price online vs. in-person, (4) Upsell and add-on options, (5) When to raise rates and how to communicate it to existing clients. Calculate: how many clients at each tier I need to hit my income goal.
Pro tip: Package pricing (not pay-per-session) improves retention, reduces cancellations, and makes your income predictable. Lead with packages.

Prompt 12 — Online Training Business Setup

I want to add online personal training to my business to scale beyond local 1-on-1 sessions. Current situation: [in-person only / some online clients / brand new] Tech comfort level: [low / medium / high] Target online clients: [describe — similar to in-person or different niche?] Income goal from online: $[amount]/month additional Plan my online training launch: (1) Minimum viable tech stack (what tools I need), (2) Service model options (1-on-1 online / group / self-paced / hybrid), (3) First 30 days action plan to get first 3 online clients, (4) How to deliver a great experience remotely, (5) Pricing strategy for online vs. in-person. Keep it lean — I don't want to build a complex tech stack.

6. Content & Education

Prompt 13 — Client Education Video Script

Write a short educational video script (60–90 seconds) for [Instagram Reels / TikTok / YouTube Shorts] on [topic: most common gym mistake / how to do X exercise / nutrition myth / recovery tips / beginner advice]. Audience: [my target niche] My style: [direct and no-nonsense / warm and encouraging / science-based / entertaining] Script format: Hook (first 3 seconds — stop the scroll) → Core content (3 key points) → Actionable takeaway → CTA. Include on-screen text suggestions. The script should sound natural when spoken, not read.

Prompt 14 — PT Certification Study Plan

I'm studying for [NASM / ACE / ISSA / NSCA / other] certification. Exam date: [date]. Study time available: [hours/week]. Current knowledge level: [beginner / some background]. Build a study plan: Week-by-week topics to cover → Key concepts to master for each domain → How to study (active recall, practice questions, application) → Resources to prioritize → The week before exam plan. Flag the domains that most people struggle with and why.

Prompt 15 — Annual Business Review

Help me review my personal training business. This year's numbers: [clients, revenue, sessions delivered, retention rate, new clients, churn] What went well: [wins — programs, clients, marketing, income] What struggled: [challenges — client churn, marketing, time, burnout, income plateau] Where I am now vs. where I want to be: [describe the gap] Analyze: (1) My biggest lever for growth next year, (2) What I should stop doing that's costing time without results, (3) A realistic revenue goal for next year with a path to get there, (4) My #1 priority for Q1, (5) The honest answer to: why haven't I already solved my biggest challenge?
