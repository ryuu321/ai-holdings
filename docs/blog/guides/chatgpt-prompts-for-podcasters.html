ChatGPT Prompts for Podcasters

15 free prompts to plan better episodes, write show notes that rank, land great guests, grow your audience, and actually make money from your podcast.

Most podcasts die at episode 20 — not because the host runs out of ideas, but because the invisible workload (show notes, promotion, guest outreach, titles) becomes unsustainable. These 15 prompts cut that overhead so you can focus on recording great content and growing a show that lasts.

1. Episode Planning & Ideas

Prompt 1 — Episode Idea Generator

I host a podcast about [niche/topic]. My audience is [describe: who they are, what they're trying to achieve]. My show's format: [solo / interview / co-hosted / storytelling]. Generate 20 episode ideas. For each: (1) Episode title (click-worthy), (2) Core premise in 1 sentence, (3) Format (solo deep-dive / interview / round-up / case study), (4) Why this would resonate with my audience now, (5) A potential guest for interview episodes. Mix: evergreen topics, trending conversations, personal stories, and listener Q&A. Flag the 5 with the highest growth potential.
Pro tip: Your best episode ideas come from listener questions and conversations. Mine your DMs, reviews, and comments first before generating ideas from scratch.

Prompt 2 — Episode Outline Builder

Build a detailed episode outline for my podcast. Episode topic: [title or subject] Format: [solo / interview / co-hosted] Target length: [20 / 45 / 60 / 90 minutes] My audience: [describe] The 1 thing I want listeners to walk away with: [the core takeaway] Outline: (1) Hook opening (first 60 seconds — what makes someone keep listening), (2) Context-setting (what are we solving/exploring today), (3) Main sections with key talking points (3–5 sections), (4) Pattern interrupt / energy reset at midpoint, (5) CTA (subscribe / review / visit / buy), (6) Outro. For interview episodes: include 8–10 interview questions with follow-up probes.

2. Show Notes & SEO

Prompt 3 — SEO Show Notes Writer

Write SEO-optimized show notes for my podcast episode. Episode title: [title] Episode summary: [what was discussed in 3–5 sentences] Key guest (if any): [name, title, credentials] Main topics covered: [list 5–7] Key quotes from the episode: [paste 2–3 strong quotes] Links mentioned: [list] Target keyword: [what people would search to find this episode] Write: (1) Episode intro paragraph (150 words, keyword-rich, compelling), (2) Key takeaways section (5–7 bullet points), (3) Time-stamped chapter markers, (4) Guest bio (if applicable), (5) Resources/links section, (6) SEO meta description (under 155 chars). The show notes should rank on Google, not just live on your podcast host.
Pro tip: Show notes that rank on Google generate listeners who never would have found you on Spotify. Treat each episode's show notes as a standalone blog post.

Prompt 4 — Episode Title Optimizer

Write 10 title options for my podcast episode about [topic]. My podcast name: [name] | Niche: [niche] Core value of this episode: [what will listeners gain] Notable guest (if any): [name] Target listener: [who this episode is for] Title formats to include: Interview format ("How [Guest] Did X") → Numbered list → Question → Bold statement → Transformation ("From X to Y") → Insider knowledge → Counter-intuitive claim → Curiosity gap → Listener benefit. Rate each for click-worthiness and searchability. Flag your top 3 picks and explain why.

3. Guest Outreach

Prompt 5 — Guest Pitch Email

Write a podcast guest pitch email. My podcast: [name] — [brief description, audience size if it helps, notable past guests] Target guest: [name, their work, why they'd be a fit] Episode angle: [what I'd interview them about specifically] Why this benefits them: [audience fit, exposure, cause they care about] Email structure: Open with something specific about their work (not generic flattery) → Describe the podcast briefly → The episode angle I have in mind → Why their audience would benefit → Simple next step (15-minute call or "interested?"). Under 200 words. Professional, direct, and human — not a form letter.
Pro tip: The best pitch emails prove you've actually consumed the guest's work. Reference a specific piece of content they created. Generic pitches are ignored instantly.

Prompt 6 — Guest Prep Package

Write a guest prep package for my podcast. My podcast: [name] — [topic, format, typical episode length] Guest: [name, background] Episode topic: [what we'll discuss] Recording platform: [Riverside / Zencastr / Zoom / etc.] Air date (if known): [date] Create: (1) Welcome email with recording logistics (warm, brief), (2) Pre-interview questionnaire (5 questions to help me personalize the interview), (3) Technical setup guide (what they need, how to prepare their audio), (4) Episode angle and 5 sample questions so they know what to expect, (5) What happens after recording (editing timeline, promotion, how I'll tag them). Make it feel organized and professional — this reflects on my brand.

4. Promotion & Growth

Prompt 7 — Episode Promotion Content Pack

Build a promotion content pack for my new podcast episode. Episode: "[title]" with [guest or topic] Key quote: [the most shareable line from the episode] Target platforms: [Instagram / Twitter/X / LinkedIn / TikTok / Facebook / email] Episode length: [minutes] Best moment timestamp: [where the best 60-second clip lives] Create: (1) Instagram caption + 5 hashtags, (2) Twitter/X thread (5 tweets), (3) LinkedIn post (professional framing), (4) Email newsletter section (150 words), (5) Story/reel text overlay for the clip, (6) Quote graphic text. Each piece should drive to the episode — not just announce it. Emphasize the insight or value, not just "new episode out."

Prompt 8 — Podcast Growth Strategy

Build a 90-day podcast growth strategy for my show. Podcast: [name] — [niche, episode count, current listener numbers] Current promotion: [what I'm doing now] Content strength: [what's performing best] Resources: [solo creator / small team, hours/week available for marketing] Growth goal: [X listeners / downloads per episode in 90 days] Strategy: (1) Low-effort high-ROI promotion wins I can do this week, (2) Guest swap / cross-promotion strategy, (3) SEO content strategy (show notes + standalone blog posts), (4) Community building tactics, (5) One "audience multiplication" experiment to run in 30 days. Prioritize actions with the highest listener-per-hour-invested ratio.
Pro tip: Podcast growth compounds through listener word-of-mouth and search discoverability. The two best investments: great episode SEO and being a guest on other shows.

5. Monetization

Prompt 9 — Sponsorship Pitch Deck

Help me create a podcast sponsorship pitch for brands. My podcast: [name] — [niche, episode count, monthly downloads, audience demographics] Engagement: [avg listen-through rate, email list size, social following] Niche fit: [what types of brands would resonate with my audience] Sponsorship options I'm offering: [pre-roll / mid-roll / episode title / exclusive content] Rate I want: $[CPM or flat rate per episode] Write: (1) One-paragraph podcast description for the pitch, (2) Audience profile (who listens and what they care about), (3) Sponsorship package descriptions (3 tiers), (4) Cold email subject line + pitch body (200 words), (5) Why [specific brand] and my audience are a natural fit (template I can customize per brand).

Prompt 10 — Premium Content & Community Strategy

Help me build a monetization strategy for my podcast beyond ads. My podcast: [name] — [niche, audience size, what they're trying to achieve] Time I have to build additional offerings: [hours/week] My audience's biggest pain point: [describe] Price points I'm considering: [$5–$15/month / $47–$97 one-time / $197–$497 course] Generate: (1) 3 membership/premium content tier ideas (what's included, pricing, rationale), (2) Digital product ideas that align with my existing content, (3) Live event or community option, (4) Which monetization path I should start with given my audience size, (5) Revenue milestone targets at 6 months / 12 months. Which model requires least effort to launch first?
Pro tip: Don't wait until you have 10K downloads to monetize. A small, highly engaged audience of 500 true fans will buy from you. Volume is overrated; depth of connection is everything.

6. Operations & Systems

Prompt 11 — Production Workflow System

Build a repeatable production workflow for my podcast. Format: [solo / interview] Current time it takes from recording to publish: [hours] Steps I currently do: [list your workflow] Bottleneck: [what takes longest or what I forget] Tools I use: [recording / editing / hosting / show notes / promotion] Design a streamlined workflow: (1) Pre-production checklist, (2) Recording checklist, (3) Post-production steps in order (with time estimates), (4) Publishing checklist, (5) Promotion checklist. Suggest 2–3 places I could save time with templates or batching. The goal: consistent quality with less mental overhead.

Prompt 12 — Listener Q&A Episode Builder

I want to do a listener Q&A episode. Here are the questions my audience submitted: [Paste 5–10 listener questions] Help me: (1) Select the 5–7 best questions (most universal, most compelling, most likely to be helpful to non-askers), (2) Group them thematically so the episode flows, (3) Write an opening hook for each answer that makes non-asking listeners care, (4) Suggest a full episode structure (title, open, question order, close, CTA), (5) Draft a 30-second teaser I can use on social before the episode drops.

7. Career & Brand

Prompt 13 — Podcast Brand Statement

Help me write a clear, compelling brand statement for my podcast. Show name: [name] What I cover: [topic/niche] Who it's for: [ideal listener — be specific] What they get from listening: [transformation or value] My hosting style: [conversational / educational / storytelling / interview] What makes my show different: [your unique angle] Write: (1) One-sentence show description (for podcast platforms), (2) Two-sentence expanded description, (3) Tagline under 10 words, (4) Full "About the Show" (100 words for show notes / website), (5) Twitter/Instagram bio version (under 150 chars). Each should sound distinct from the generic "join us as we explore..." template.

Prompt 14 — Podcast Repurposing System

I want to repurpose my podcast episodes across platforms. I publish [X episodes/month]. Current platforms I'm active on: [list] Time I have for repurposing: [hours/week] Episodes I most want to repurpose: [evergreen / interview highlights / solo deep-dives] Build a repurposing system: For each episode type, what to extract and where to publish it. Include: short-form video (Reels/TikTok), blog post, email, Twitter thread, LinkedIn, and quote graphics. Create a decision tree: "If the episode is X type, the repurposing priority is Y." I want a system I can delegate or repeat — not a one-off list.

Prompt 15 — Annual Podcast Review

Help me review my podcast year. Episodes published: [number] | Average downloads per episode: [number] Best-performing episodes: [titles + download counts] Episodes that underperformed: [titles + why you think they flopped] Biggest win: [describe] Biggest frustration: [describe] Revenue generated (if any): [amount and source] Where I am vs. where I wanted to be: [honest gap analysis] Analyze: (1) What pattern do my top episodes share? (2) What should I stop doing — content, format, frequency? (3) The #1 lever for growth in the next year, (4) My sustainable publishing schedule given my energy (not my ambition), (5) What would need to be true for this to become a significant income source.
