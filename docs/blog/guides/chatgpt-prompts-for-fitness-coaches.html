15 ChatGPT Prompts for Fitness Coaches

Stop spending hours on program design, check-in messages, and social content. Use AI to handle the paperwork so you can focus on coaching.

The best fitness coaches aren't always the most knowledgeable about exercise science — they're the ones who show up consistently, communicate clearly, and make clients feel supported. These prompts help you do all three at scale, whether you have 5 clients or 50.

These work for personal trainers, online fitness coaches, group fitness instructors, nutrition coaches, and hybrid coaches. Always apply your professional judgment before delivering any program or nutrition guidance to clients.

Program Design

1. The Client Program Template

Writing individualized programs from scratch for every client is the biggest time drain in coaching. This prompt creates a solid starting framework.

Create a 4-week beginner/intermediate workout program for this client: Age: [X]. Goal: [lose weight / build muscle / improve fitness / sport-specific / general health]. Training days available: [X days/week]. Available equipment: [gym with full equipment / home with dumbbells / bodyweight only / etc.]. Injuries or limitations: [list any — or "none"]. Current fitness level: [describe briefly]. Structure the program as: Week 1-2: Foundation phase (focus on form, build work capacity). Week 3-4: Progression phase (increase intensity). For each day: exercise name, sets, reps/time, rest, and coaching cue (one sentence on the most important technique point). Include a deload option at week 4. NOTE: This is a starting framework — I will review and adjust before sending to the client.

2. The Progressive Overload Planner

Help me plan the progression for this client over 12 weeks. Client details: [describe — goal, current performance on key lifts or cardio metrics, training history]. Current benchmarks: [list their current weights/times/distances on 3-5 key exercises]. Goal by week 12: [what do you want them to achieve?]. Build a progression plan that: 1) Shows week-by-week targets for each key lift/metric, 2) Identifies which weeks to deload (typically every 4th week), 3) Flags which exercises to progress aggressively vs. conservatively based on their history, 4) Includes a note on what to do if they miss a session or a week. Format as a simple table I can reference each week when writing their sessions.

3. The Nutrition Framework Builder

Create a basic nutrition framework for this client (not a meal plan — a set of principles and practical guidelines). Client: [goal, lifestyle, dietary restrictions, cooking ability — "hates cooking" is relevant, current eating patterns]. Goal: [body composition, performance, health]. Write a framework covering: 1) Rough daily calorie and protein targets (show your math), 2) Meal timing guidelines that fit their schedule, 3) 5 practical food swaps for their most common unhealthy habits, 4) Pre/post workout nutrition guidelines (simple, not complicated), 5) What to do on days when the plan falls apart (not "start over Monday" — actual strategies). NOTE: For clients with medical conditions, refer to a registered dietitian. This is a general wellness framework only.

Client Communication & Retention

4. The Weekly Check-In Template

Consistent check-ins are the #1 predictor of client retention. Most coaches make them too long and clients stop filling them in.

Write a weekly client check-in form that takes under 3 minutes to complete. The check-in should capture: 1) Adherence (workouts completed, nutrition compliance — simple 1-5 scale), 2) The one biggest win from the week, 3) The one biggest struggle, 4) Energy and recovery rating (1-10), 5) One question for their coach. Also write: A follow-up message template I send after reviewing their check-in (under 50 words — acknowledge what they said, give one specific action item, end with encouragement that feels real, not generic). The goal: check-ins that take 3 minutes to complete and 5 minutes for me to respond to, but make clients feel genuinely supported.

5. The Client Who Wants to Quit Conversation

A client is thinking about canceling. Situation: [describe — they've gone quiet / they told you directly / they've missed sessions / they said they "can't afford it right now" / they're not seeing results]. Their history with me: [how long, what progress they've made]. Write: 1) A message to send that acknowledges what they've said and opens a conversation, 2) What to say in a check-in call to understand the real reason (it's rarely the stated reason), 3) A retention offer that makes sense (not desperation discounting — real value), 4) How to let them go gracefully if they still want to leave (this is how you get referrals and re-signs). Give me the actual words, not coaching advice about what to do.

6. The Milestone Celebration System

Build a milestone celebration system for my coaching business. Types of milestones my clients hit: [list — first workout completed, first 5 lbs lost, first unassisted pull-up, 3-month anniversary, etc.]. Write: 1) A celebration message template for each milestone type (personalized enough to feel genuine, efficient enough to send in 2 minutes), 2) A milestone tracking sheet I can use to make sure I notice every win, 3) A monthly "highlight" message format — something I can send to long-term clients that references their journey, 4) How to turn milestone moments into social proof (with client permission) — what to ask, how to ask it. Clients who feel celebrated stay longer and refer more.

Marketing & Client Acquisition

7. The Social Media Content Plan

Build a 4-week social media content plan for a fitness coach in [your niche: weight loss / strength / online coaching / functional fitness / etc.]. Platform: [Instagram / TikTok / Facebook / all]. Posting frequency: [X times/week]. Content goals: [build trust / attract new clients / educate audience / all]. Build the plan with post types: Educational (technique tips, myth-busting, nutrition info), Social proof (client wins, transformations, testimonials), Personal/behind the scenes, Promotional (your services, offers). For each post: topic, format (Reel, static, carousel, Story), hook for the caption, CTA. Also: write 3 "evergreen" posts I can reuse monthly that never go stale.

8. The Transformation Story Post

Write a social media post sharing a client transformation story. Client details (anonymized or with permission): [describe their starting point, what they struggled with, what changed]. Results: [specific — pounds lost, strength gained, energy improved, habit built — not just "amazing transformation"]. My coaching approach: [what specifically did you do that made the difference?]. Write: 1) An Instagram caption (under 300 words, starts with their struggle not their result — lead with empathy), 2) A shorter version for Facebook (under 150 words), 3) 3 different hooks I can test (first line only), 4) A Story script (5-7 slides, conversational). Always center the client as the hero — my role is supporting, not the focus.

9. The Discovery Call Script

Write a 20-minute discovery call script for potential coaching clients. My coaching offer: [describe — program type, duration, price, what's included]. Target client: [describe who you help best]. Write a call structure that: 1) Opens with a question about them (not "let me tell you about my program"), 2) Uncovers their real goal and what's been stopping them, 3) Qualifies whether they're a good fit (for both of us), 4) Presents my program as the solution to what they just told me, 5) Handles the 3 most common objections: "I need to think about it," "It's too expensive," "I'm not sure I'm ready", 6) Closes with a clear next step. Give me word-for-word language for each section, not just an outline.

Business Operations

10. The Onboarding Welcome Package

Write the copy for a client onboarding welcome package. My coaching program: [describe — duration, format, what's included, how it works]. Write: 1) Welcome email (sent immediately after payment — warm, excited, clear next steps), 2) "What to expect in week 1" message, 3) Community guidelines (if you have a group), 4) FAQ section (5 most common questions new clients have — include answers), 5) A "this is how to get the most out of working with me" section (what clients who get the best results do differently). Goal: clients feel they made the right decision and know exactly what to do next. Reduce "what do I do now?" messages by 80%.

11. The Group Program Launch Email Sequence

Write a 5-email launch sequence for my group coaching program. Program details: [name, duration, price, what's included, start date, spots available]. Target audience: [describe]. Write: Email 1 (7 days before close): Announcement + who it's for + the transformation they'll get. Email 2 (5 days before): Address the #1 objection your audience has. Email 3 (3 days before): Client story or results (with permission). Email 4 (1 day before): Urgency — spots closing, what they'll miss. Email 5 (close day): Last chance — simple, direct, no tricks. For each email: subject line (give 2 options) + body (under 250 words). Tone: confident and warm, not pushy.

12. The Testimonial Collection System

Build a system for collecting strong client testimonials. The problem with most testimonials: they're vague ("great coach!") and don't help potential clients make a decision. Build a system that: 1) The exact email to send to happy clients asking for a testimonial, 2) 5 specific questions that produce detailed, useful testimonials (about their before state, the process, specific results, what surprised them), 3) A follow-up if they don't respond in 5 days, 4) How to use testimonials across my marketing (website, social, sales calls, emails), 5) A video testimonial request script for clients who are willing to go on camera. The goal: testimonials that sell my program better than I can.

Content & Education

13. The Educational Content Generator

Generate a month of educational content topics for a [fitness niche] coach. My audience: [describe — who follows you, their fitness level, main struggles]. My expertise areas: [list your strongest topics]. Create: 1) 8 Instagram/TikTok Reel topics with a hook for each, 2) 4 longer-form post topics (carousel or blog) that demonstrate expertise, 3) 2 "myth-busting" topics that challenge common misconceptions in my niche, 4) 1 "behind the scenes" idea that builds connection, 5) For each topic: the hook (first 3 seconds or first sentence), the key takeaway, and a CTA that fits naturally. Prioritize topics that attract my ideal client, not just get views from people who'll never buy.

14. The Workout Demo Script

Write a script for a workout demonstration video for [exercise name]. Format: [Instagram Reel / YouTube video / TikTok]. Length: [15s / 30s / 60s / 2-3 min]. Audience: [beginner / intermediate / advanced]. Write a script that: 1) Hook (first 3 seconds — what problem does this solve for the viewer?), 2) The setup: equipment needed, starting position, key setup cues, 3) The movement: what to do, breathing, tempo, 4) The most common mistake and how to fix it, 5) A modification for beginners and a progression for advanced, 6) CTA (follows, saves, or DM for a program — pick the one action you want). Keep the language clear and non-jargon — talk like you're explaining it to a friend.

15. The Email Newsletter for Fitness Coaches

Write a monthly email newsletter for my fitness coaching audience. Subscriber list: [describe — past clients, leads, social media followers who opted in]. My coaching niche: [describe]. This month's theme: [a topic relevant to your niche — motivation, nutrition, plateau-busting, etc.]. Write a newsletter that: 1) Opens with a personal observation or story (2-3 sentences — real, not "motivational"), 2) Educational section: one practical tip they can use this week (specific, not "eat more protein"), 3) Client spotlight (if applicable — describe what to include), 4) Soft promotion: 1-2 sentences about your current offer or program, 5) Closes with a question that invites replies. Under 400 words. Also write 3 subject line options.
Pro tip: Start with Prompt 4 (Weekly Check-In Template). Consistent check-ins are the single highest-leverage action for client retention. A client who checks in weekly is 3x less likely to cancel than one who doesn't — and AI-assisted responses mean you can actually sustain it across a full client roster.
