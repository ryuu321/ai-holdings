ChatGPT Prompts for Amazon Sellers

15 free prompts to write better listings, find winning products, handle reviews, and scale your Amazon FBA or FBM business with AI.

Amazon is a data game. The sellers winning in 2026 are using AI to research faster, write listings that convert, respond to customers professionally, and find product opportunities before the market gets crowded. These 15 prompts give you the same leverage — without the expensive software.

1. Product Research & Validation

Prompt 1 — Product Opportunity Analyzer

I'm researching Amazon product opportunities in [category/niche]. Help me evaluate this product idea: [describe the product]. Analyze: (1) Likely market demand signals (what would indicate strong demand), (2) Competition assessment — what makes a competitor strong vs. beatable on Amazon, (3) Margin considerations — typical landed costs, FBA fees, and healthy margin targets, (4) Risk factors — commoditization, seasonality, fragility, IP issues, regulation, (5) Differentiation angle — how could I make a version that wins without being the cheapest? Rate this opportunity 1–10 and explain.
Pro tip: Use this prompt to stress-test product ideas before committing to sourcing. AI can't pull live data — always verify with Jungle Scout, Helium 10, or Amazon itself.

Prompt 2 — Niche Product Brainstorm

I sell on Amazon FBA. I'm looking for my next product in [broad category or "any category"]. My constraints: - Budget to launch: $[amount] - Preferred price point: $[low]–$[high] - Product size: [standard size / small / large — for FBA fee management] - Risk tolerance: [conservative / moderate / aggressive] - Things I want to avoid: [fragile / electrical / regulated / highly competitive] Generate 10 product ideas with: Product name → Target keyword → Why this could work → Main risk → What differentiation looks like. Prioritize ideas with low competition, clear demand signals, and healthy margins.

2. Listing Optimization

Prompt 3 — Full Amazon Listing Writer

Write a fully optimized Amazon product listing for: Product: [name and description] Category: [Amazon category] Target customer: [who buys this and why] Key features: [list 5–7 features] Unique selling points vs. competitors: [what makes yours better] Primary keyword: [main keyword] Secondary keywords: [list 3–5] Write: (1) Title (200 chars max, keyword-rich, readable), (2) 5 bullet points (start each with a bold benefit, include keywords naturally, address buyer objections), (3) Product description (1000 chars, story-led, benefits-focused, includes secondary keywords), (4) Backend search terms suggestions (not visible to buyers — maximize keyword coverage without repeating title/bullets). Do not keyword-stuff. Write for humans who also satisfy the algorithm.
Pro tip: Put your most important keyword in the first 80 characters of the title. Amazon weights early title placement heavily in search ranking.

Prompt 4 — Listing A/B Test Variants

I want to A/B test my Amazon listing for [product]. My current conversion rate is [X%] — I want to improve it. Current title: [paste current title] Current bullet points: [paste bullets] Write 2 alternative versions to test: Version A: Focus on emotional benefit / lifestyle outcome — speaks to desire Version B: Focus on problem solved / pain point — speaks to frustration For each: Full title + 5 bullets. Keep keywords intact. Change the angle, not the facts. Tell me what hypothesis each version is testing and what a "winning" result looks like.

3. Review Management

Prompt 5 — Review Request Email Sequence

Write a 2-email follow-up sequence for Amazon buyers (compliant with Amazon's communication guidelines — no review incentives, no asking for positive reviews only, no external links). Product: [what they bought] Brand tone: [friendly / professional / helpful] Email 1 (send 3–5 days after delivery): Check-in on product satisfaction + usage tips that improve their experience. Email 2 (send 10–14 days after delivery): Genuine ask for honest feedback. Both emails must: Be non-manipulative → Feel helpful, not salesy → Comply with Amazon's buyer-seller messaging policy → Include a subject line under 70 chars. Flag anything that could violate Amazon TOS.
Pro tip: The best review-generation strategy is a great product with great packaging. These emails reinforce a good experience — they don't create one.

Prompt 6 — Negative Review Response

A customer left this negative review on my Amazon product: Review: "[paste the review]" Stars given: [number] My product: [describe what it does] Was the complaint valid? [yes / partially / no — explain] Write a professional public response (Amazon allows seller responses to reviews): (1) Acknowledge their experience without being defensive, (2) Explain or clarify if appropriate, (3) Offer a resolution, (4) Keep it under 200 words, (5) Sound like a real person, not a corporate template. The response is public — write to convince future buyers, not just this reviewer.

4. PPC & Advertising

Prompt 7 — Amazon PPC Keyword Strategy

Build an Amazon PPC keyword strategy for my product: [name and description]. My goal: [launch phase / scale phase / defensive (brand protection)] Budget: $[daily budget] Current ACoS target: [percentage or "not sure"] Main keyword I already rank for: [if any] Generate: (1) 20 high-intent keywords organized by match type (exact / phrase / broad), (2) 10 negative keywords to exclude waste, (3) Campaign structure recommendation (auto vs. manual, how many campaigns), (4) Bidding strategy for launch vs. scale, (5) How to mine search term reports for new keyword opportunities. Flag any keywords likely to be too competitive for my budget.
Pro tip: Run auto campaigns for the first 2 weeks to let Amazon find your converting keywords. Then mine the search term report and move winners to exact match manual campaigns.

Prompt 8 — Product Launch Strategy

I'm launching a new Amazon product. Help me build a launch strategy. Product: [name, category, price] Competition level: [low / medium / high — based on your research] Launch budget: $[amount] Timeline: [launch date or "ASAP"] Existing assets: [brand website / email list / social following / Facebook group / none] Plan: (1) Pre-launch (2 weeks before) — how to build initial velocity, (2) Launch week — PPC structure, pricing strategy, inventory targets, (3) Review acquisition strategy (Amazon-compliant), (4) How to achieve early ranking for my primary keyword, (5) What success looks like at 30 / 60 / 90 days. Be realistic about what's achievable given my assets.

5. Customer Service

Prompt 9 — Customer Message Templates

Write Amazon seller messaging templates for these common situations. Tone: [professional / warm / helpful]. Product: [describe generally]. Templates needed: 1. Item arrived damaged — apology + resolution offer 2. Where is my order? (WISMO) — before delivery window closes 3. Product doesn't work as expected — troubleshooting + offer 4. Requesting a return — instructions + make it easy 5. Wrong item received — immediate apology + fix 6. Thank you for a 5-star review (without incentivizing future reviews) 7. Counterfeit claim (serious — needs careful language) Each template: Under 150 words, sounds human, stays Amazon-compliant. Flag any that need legal review before using.

Prompt 10 — Amazon A-to-Z Claim Response

A buyer filed an Amazon A-to-Z Guarantee claim against my order. Here are the details: Order date: [date] | Item: [product] | Claim reason: [buyer's stated reason] What actually happened: [your version of events] Evidence I have: [tracking, communications, etc.] Did the customer contact me first? [yes/no — what happened] Help me write a response to Amazon that: (1) Presents the facts clearly and professionally, (2) Includes evidence I should attach, (3) Makes the case for why the claim should be denied or why I'm already resolving it, (4) Stays under 500 words, (5) Maintains a cooperative tone with Amazon (not adversarial). This affects my account health — help me get this right.

6. Brand & Business Development

Prompt 11 — Competitor Listing Analysis

I want to analyze my top competitor's Amazon listing to find opportunities. Competitor's listing: [paste their title, bullets, and description] Their star rating: [number] and review count: [number] Top complaints in their reviews: [paste 3–5 negative review excerpts] Top praises in their reviews: [paste 3–5 positive review excerpts] Analyze: (1) What keywords are they targeting? (2) What benefits are they emphasizing? (3) What buyer pain points do negative reviews reveal that I could solve in my product or listing? (4) What positive attributes could I match or exceed? (5) What's the #1 differentiation I should build into my listing to position against this competitor?
Pro tip: Negative competitor reviews are a goldmine. They tell you exactly what customers want that they aren't getting — build that into your product or listing.

Prompt 12 — Brand Story & A+ Content

Write brand story and A+ content copy for my Amazon brand. Brand name: [name] What we sell: [category] Brand origin/mission: [why you started / what you believe in] Target customer: [describe] What makes us different: [your actual differentiators] Write: (1) Brand story (200 words — personal, specific, not generic), (2) 3 A+ Content module headlines with supporting copy (each module: 100 words), (3) Comparison chart headers (for showing your product vs. competitors without naming them), (4) FAQ section (5 questions + answers). Tone: [premium / approachable / technical / lifestyle].

7. Operations & Scaling

Prompt 13 — Supplier Negotiation Script

Help me prepare to negotiate with a manufacturer/supplier on Alibaba or at a trade show. Product I'm sourcing: [describe] Current sample quality: [acceptable / issues to address] Target unit cost: $[price] (currently quoted at $[their price]) Minimum order quantity they require: [MOQ] My leverage: [describe — cash upfront, repeat business potential, other quotes] Build my negotiation strategy: (1) Opening message or conversation opener, (2) How to negotiate on price without damaging the relationship, (3) What to ask for besides price (payment terms, lead times, packaging customization, samples), (4) Red flags that suggest I should walk away, (5) How to confirm quality standards in writing.

Prompt 14 — Inventory Management Planning

Help me plan my Amazon inventory for the next 90 days. Current inventory: [units in stock / in transit] Daily sales velocity: [units/day] Lead time from supplier: [weeks] FBA storage limits or fees I'm watching: [describe or "not an issue"] Upcoming events that affect demand: [Prime Day / holiday / seasonal spike / none] Calculate: (1) Reorder point (when to place my next order), (2) Reorder quantity given lead time and velocity, (3) Safety stock to protect against velocity spikes, (4) Maximum inventory to avoid long-term storage fees, (5) Cash tied up in inventory at each scenario. Flag any stockout risk I should act on now.
Pro tip: Running out of stock kills your ranking. Build 30 days of safety stock beyond your reorder point until your sales are predictable.

Prompt 15 — Amazon Business Annual Review

Help me review my Amazon business. This year: [revenue, units sold, profit margin, number of SKUs, best sellers, underperformers] What worked: [product launches, PPC strategies, listing improvements] What didn't: [failures, wasted spend, stockouts, account issues] Where I am vs. my goal: [gap analysis] Amazon landscape changes that affected me: [algorithm, fee changes, competition] Analyze: (1) My most profitable SKU and why — can I do more of this? (2) Which products I should discontinue and why, (3) Where I'm leaving money on the table, (4) My top 3 priorities for next year, (5) The one thing holding my business back that I keep avoiding.
