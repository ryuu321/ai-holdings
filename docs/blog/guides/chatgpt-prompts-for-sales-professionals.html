ChatGPT Prompts for Sales Professionals

15 free prompts to write better cold emails, handle objections with confidence, close more deals, and build a high-income sales career — with AI as your thinking partner.

The best salespeople aren't just persuasive — they're prepared. They know the prospect's pain before the call, they have objection responses ready, they follow up at the right time with the right message. These 15 prompts help you prepare faster, write better, and spend more time in conversations and less time on admin.

1. Prospecting & Outreach

Prompt 1 — Cold Email Sequence

Write a 4-email cold outreach sequence for [target prospect type: VP of Sales / CEO of SMB / HR director / CFO / etc.]. What I sell: [brief description] The problem I solve for this persona: [be specific — not generic "save time"] My proof point: [stat, case study result, or customer outcome] CTA: [15-minute call / demo / reply with interest] Tone: [direct / conversational / consultative] Email 1 (day 1): Lead with their problem, not my product. Ultra-short — 5 sentences max. Email 2 (day 3): Different angle — lead with a relevant insight or stat. Email 3 (day 7): Social proof — brief customer success story (anonymized if needed). Email 4 (day 14): "Last touch" — graceful exit that leaves the door open. Subject lines for each. No more than 75 words per email body. Respect their time.
Pro tip: The best cold emails are shorter than you think. 5 sentences that prove you understand their problem outperforms 5 paragraphs about your product every time.

Prompt 2 — LinkedIn Prospecting Messages

Write LinkedIn outreach messages for prospecting. Target: [job title / company type / industry] What I sell: [brief description] Connection request strategy: [personalized note or blank — what works in my industry] Write 5 different LinkedIn message sequences: (1) Post engagement → DM (they liked your post), (2) Shared group → DM, (3) Company trigger → DM (they just hired a new team / raised funding / launched a product), (4) Cold DM — no prior connection, (5) Referral introduction. For each: Connection message (under 300 chars) + follow-up DM (under 150 words). No "I'd love to pick your brain." No "I noticed your profile." Real, specific, human.

2. Discovery & Qualification

Prompt 3 — Discovery Call Question Set

Write a discovery call question set for qualifying and understanding prospects. What I sell: [product/service] Typical prospect: [job title, company size, industry] My sales cycle: [transactional / consultative / enterprise] Decision maker dynamic: [single DM / buying committee / economic buyer + champion] Key qualification criteria: [what makes a good vs. bad fit] Build 3 question sets: (1) Situation questions (understand their current state — 4–6 questions) (2) Problem/implication questions (help them articulate and feel the problem — 4–6 questions) (3) Need-payoff questions (get them to sell themselves on the value — 3–4 questions) For each question: the question itself + what a good answer looks like + what a red flag sounds like. This is for prep, not a script — I'll adapt in the conversation.
Pro tip: The rep who asks the best questions wins. The prospect who articulates their own pain and desired outcome is already half-sold before you present anything.

Prompt 4 — Account Research Brief

Help me build a pre-call research brief for a prospect. Company: [name] | Industry: [type] | Size: [employees / revenue if known] Contact: [name / title] What I know: [any intel — recent news, LinkedIn activity, job postings, tech stack, trigger events] What I sell: [brief description] My goal for this call: [qualify / advance to demo / close] Build a research brief: (1) Company situation summary (2–3 sentences), (2) Likely pain points based on their industry/size/role, (3) Trigger events to reference (growth signals, challenges, news), (4) Connection points (mutual contacts, shared background, their recent content), (5) Opening question or observation tailored to this specific prospect. This prep takes 5 minutes but changes the entire conversation.

3. Objection Handling

Prompt 5 — Objection Response Library

Build a comprehensive objection response library for what I sell. Product/service: [describe] Price point: $[amount] Write responses for these objections: (1) "It's too expensive / not in our budget", (2) "We're happy with our current vendor", (3) "I need to think about it", (4) "Send me some information and I'll look it over", (5) "Now isn't a good time", (6) "We handle this internally", (7) "I need to get approval from [person]", (8) "Can you match [competitor's] price?", (9) "We're too small / too early for this", (10) "I've heard mixed reviews." For each: Acknowledge → Explore (ask the right question) → Reframe → Advance. These are conversation scripts, not arguments. The goal is understanding, not winning.

Prompt 6 — Stalled Deal Revival

Help me revive stalled deals in my pipeline. Deal context: [describe: stage it's stuck at, how long it's been stalled, what happened last] What I know about the prospect's situation: [any updates] Why it may have stalled: [choose or describe: champion left / budget frozen / competitor / lost urgency / decision delayed] Write revival approaches for each stall reason: (1) Champion gone — how to find a new one, (2) Budget frozen — how to reframe ROI or timing, (3) Competitor threat — how to differentiate without badmouthing, (4) Lost urgency — how to re-create it without pressure tactics, (5) Just gone quiet — best "have you found what you needed?" message. Include email + phone script for each.
Pro tip: Stalled deals almost always have an unstated reason. The "breakup email" (permission to walk away) triggers more honest conversations than another follow-up asking "any update?"

4. Proposals & Closing

Prompt 7 — Executive Proposal

Write an executive summary for a sales proposal. Company I'm proposing to: [name] | Contact: [title] The problem they described: [exact language they used if possible] My solution: [what I'm proposing and how it solves their stated problem] Expected outcomes: [ROI, metrics, timeline] Investment: $[amount / structure] Differentiator: [why us vs. alternatives] Executive summary (1 page): (1) Their situation in their words (shows I listened), (2) What we're proposing — specific to their situation (not a product sheet), (3) Expected outcomes — quantified if possible, (4) Why us (without bashing competitors), (5) Next step with specific date. Executives make decisions in 30 seconds — this page is everything.

Prompt 8 — Closing Conversation Scripts

Write closing scripts for different deal situations. What I sell: [product/service] | My typical deal size: $[amount] Scripts for: (1) Trial close after demo — gauge where they are without pressure, (2) Assumptive close — appropriate after clear buying signals, (3) Summary close — "based on what you told me, here's what I'm recommending..." (4) Urgency close — real deadline (not fake), (5) "What would need to be true for you to move forward?", (6) Response to "let us think it over" — advance without pressure, (7) Handling "the price is too high" at the close. For each: The exact language + body language notes (if in-person) + what to do if they don't respond the way you hoped. Closing is a skill, not a trick.
Pro tip: The best close isn't a line — it's a series of mini-commitments throughout the entire sales process. If you nail discovery and proposal alignment, the close is just asking for what they already agreed to.

5. Account Management & Expansion

Prompt 9 — Customer Success Check-In

Write customer success check-in emails for different stages of the customer relationship. What I sell: [product/service] | Customer type: [describe] Write 5 check-in email templates: (1) 30 days post-onboarding — usage check, early value confirmation, (2) 90 days — mid-contract check, identify any friction, (3) Pre-renewal — 60–90 days before renewal — proactive conversation, (4) After a support ticket was resolved — ensure satisfaction, (5) After a customer achieves a notable success using your product — celebrate and capture testimonial. Each: Under 150 words, warm and specific. Check-in emails are retention tools — don't make them feel like account reviews. Make them feel like a colleague genuinely checking in.

Prompt 10 — Upsell & Expansion Emails

Write expansion and upsell emails for existing customers. What I sell: [product/service] Upsell opportunity: [upgrade / additional seat / add-on product / expanded contract] Customer situation: [they've been using for X months / they've hit a usage limit / they mentioned a new problem] The value of the upsell: [what it unlocks for them] Write 3 expansion emails: (1) Triggered by usage behavior (they're at 80% capacity — right time to expand), (2) Triggered by a new use case they mentioned, (3) Renewal conversation expansion — "as we renew, here's what I'd recommend." Each: Frame the conversation around their growth, not your quota. The best upsells feel like advice, not sales.

6. Sales Development & Career

Prompt 11 — SDR/BDR Cold Call Script

Write a cold call script for a sales development rep (SDR/BDR). Target: [prospect job title] What we sell: [brief description] Goal of the call: [book a meeting — not close a deal] Common gatekeeper scenarios: [direct to voicemail / screened by admin / direct answer] Typical objections: [not interested / no budget / wrong person / send an email] Script: (1) Gatekeeper handling, (2) Cold call opener (5 seconds to earn 30 seconds), (3) Value proposition in 15 seconds, (4) Permission-based bridge ("is this even on your radar?"), (5) Meeting ask — specific, easy, low-risk, (6) Voicemail script (under 20 seconds), (7) Handling "just send me an email" (how to still get the meeting). Role-play script format with both sides of the conversation.
Pro tip: The cold call opener is a pattern interrupt, not a pitch. "The reason for my call is..." works better than "Did I catch you at a bad time?" — which invites them to say yes.

Prompt 12 — Sales Interview Prep

Help me prepare for a sales job interview. Role: [AE / SDR / Enterprise AE / Sales Manager / VP of Sales] Company type: [startup / scale-up / enterprise / SaaS / services] My background: [years of experience, industries, deal types, quota attainment] What I'm weakest at: [be honest] Prepare me for: (1) "Walk me through your sales process" — specific, outcome-focused answer, (2) "Tell me about a deal you lost and what you learned" — shows self-awareness, (3) "How do you handle a prospect who goes cold?" — shows resilience and skill, (4) "What's your quota attainment been?" — how to frame good and bad years, (5) "Sell me this pen / our product" — how to approach a live roleplay. Full sample answers I can adapt.

Prompt 13 — Sales Methodology Primer

Explain [sales methodology] to me and help me apply it to what I sell. Methodology: [MEDDIC / MEDDPICC / Challenger / SPIN / Sandler / Gap Selling / Command of the Message] What I sell: [description] My typical sales cycle: [transactional / 30-day / 6-month enterprise] My biggest challenge: [finding urgency / accessing economic buyers / differentiating / handling objections] Explain: (1) Core principles in plain language, (2) How this methodology addresses my specific challenge, (3) 3 real scenarios where I'd apply it in my deals, (4) The 1 thing from this methodology most sellers misapply, (5) My #1 daily habit to ingrain this into my practice. Make it practical, not theoretical.

Prompt 14 — Weekly Sales Review

Help me run my weekly sales review. This week's results: [calls made, emails sent, meetings booked, demos delivered, proposals sent, deals closed] Pipeline status: [open opportunities, total value, average deal age] Quota attainment this month: [%] What I did well: [describe] What didn't work: [be specific — not "I need to work harder"] Biggest deal at risk: [stage, why it's at risk] Analyze: (1) My conversion rate at each stage — where am I losing deals? (2) The pattern in my wins this week, (3) My #1 priority next week, (4) The specific action on my most at-risk deal, (5) What I'll do differently Monday morning.

Prompt 15 — Annual Sales Career Review

Help me review my sales career this year. Quota attainment: [%] | Deals closed: [number] | Average deal size: $[amount] Best deal and why it worked: [describe] Worst loss and what you learned: [honest] Sales skills I built: [specific] Sales skills I avoided: [be honest — what am I not good at yet?] Income this year: $[amount] | Income goal next year: $[amount] Analyze: (1) What's driving my best deals — can I replicate the pattern? (2) The skill gap most likely to limit my income growth, (3) Whether I'm in the right role/company for where I want to go, (4) The 3 specific skills or habits to develop in Q1, (5) The honest answer to: what's the real reason I'm not at the top of my leaderboard?
