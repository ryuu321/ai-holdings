ChatGPT Prompts for Insurance Agents

15 free prompts to write better client emails, handle objections, generate referrals, create educational content, and grow your insurance book of business with AI.

Insurance is a relationships business built on trust, clarity, and consistent follow-through. AI can't replace the human element — but it can help you write faster, follow up more consistently, and educate clients in a way that builds the kind of trust that generates referrals. These 15 prompts are your starting point.

Important: All client communications must comply with state insurance regulations and your carrier or agency's compliance policies. Review AI-generated content before use, and never input client personal or policy information into AI tools.

1. Client Communication

Prompt 1 — Policy Review Outreach

Write an email to invite existing clients to an annual policy review. Agent: [name] | Agency: [name] | Lines I sell: [auto / home / life / commercial / all] Client type: [personal lines / business / mixed] Why now: [renewal season / life event trigger / regulatory change / just good practice] Tone: [professional / warm / neighborly] Email: Subject line (curiosity or benefit-focused — not "Annual Review Reminder") → Brief opening that acknowledges the relationship → Why this conversation matters to them specifically → What we'll cover in 20 minutes → Easy scheduling CTA. Under 200 words. Don't sound like you're trying to upsell — sound like you're doing your job as their advisor. The sale happens naturally in the review conversation.
Pro tip: Clients who do annual reviews have 2–3x higher retention rates. Frame the review as something you do FOR them, not something they need to do for you.

Prompt 2 — Coverage Explanation Email

Write a client email explaining [coverage type: umbrella liability / uninsured motorist / replacement cost vs. ACV / life insurance cash value / business interruption / errors and omissions / flood insurance]. Client situation: [they just bought this coverage / they asked what it covers / they're questioning why they need it] My goal: [help them understand the value / prevent them from dropping this coverage] Write: Subject line + email body (200 words). Use one concrete example (a scenario they can picture) to illustrate when the coverage matters. No jargon. End with: "Call me if you have questions" or a specific next step. Accurate coverage explanation is a compliance and retention tool — write it to be both clear and defensible.

Prompt 3 — Claims Process Guidance Email

Write an email to walk a client through the claims process. Claim type: [auto accident / home damage / theft / liability / business interruption / etc.] Client situation: [just filed / about to file / asking what to do] My role: [independent agent who advocates for them / captive agent] Write: What to do immediately → What documentation to gather → What to expect in the process (timeline, adjuster contact) → What I'm doing on their behalf → How to reach me with questions. Under 300 words. Calm and organized — clients in a claims situation are already stressed. Your email should feel like having a knowledgeable friend guiding them, not a form letter.
Pro tip: Clients who feel supported during a claim are your most loyal advocates. The claim experience — handled well — is the best retention and referral tool you have.

2. Sales & Prospecting

Prompt 4 — Cold Outreach Scripts

Write prospecting outreach for [target niche: new homeowners / new parents / small business owners / new drivers / retirees / real estate investors / contractors]. My lines: [what I sell that's relevant to this niche] Channel: [email / LinkedIn / handwritten note / phone script] My differentiator: [what makes working with me better than buying online] Local market: [city or region] Write: (1) Email version (150 words — specific to their life stage or event, not generic), (2) Phone script opener (30 seconds — acknowledge they're busy, state value, ask for 5 minutes), (3) LinkedIn message (under 100 words), (4) Handwritten note (under 60 words — for highest-value prospects). Each must feel personal. The best prospecting shows you understand their situation before asking for anything.

Prompt 5 — Objection Handling Scripts

Write scripts for handling the most common insurance sales objections. My lines: [what I sell] | My typical prospect: [describe] Write responses for: (1) "I'm already covered through [company/online]" — show why advisor relationship matters, (2) "It's too expensive" — reframe value, don't just cut price, (3) "I need to think about it" — understand the real hesitation, (4) "I'll just go online" — differentiate service vs. self-service, (5) "I don't need that much coverage" — education without fear tactics, (6) "I'll wait until [event] to get coverage" — address procrastination gently. For each: Acknowledge their point → Reframe → Ask a clarifying question or make the case → Soft close. Don't argue. These are conversations, not debates.

Prompt 6 — Referral Request Scripts

Write referral request scripts for different situations. My lines: [what I sell] | My best clients: [describe] Scripts for: (1) After a smooth policy delivery — while they're happy (email version), (2) After a claims experience where I helped them — the best moment (phone script), (3) Annual review follow-up — "who else like you should I be talking to?", (4) LinkedIn post that invites referrals without being awkward, (5) Referral partner (real estate agent / mortgage broker / CPA) introduction script. Each script: Makes asking for a referral feel natural, not transactional. Remind them what problem I solve → Thank them → Make the ask specific (not "anyone you know") → Make it easy to refer.
Pro tip: "Who do you know who just bought a house / had a baby / started a business?" converts 3x better than "anyone you know who needs insurance." Specific triggers > general asks.

3. Educational Content

Prompt 7 — Client Newsletter

Write a monthly client newsletter for an insurance agent. My lines: [what I sell] | Client type: [personal / commercial / mixed] This month's theme: [seasonal / life stage / a specific coverage type / industry news] Compliance constraint: No specific product recommendations without individual underwriting. Newsletter sections: (1) Personal note (50 words — builds relationship, not about insurance), (2) Education piece — one coverage concept explained in plain language (200 words, no jargon), (3) "Did you know?" — one surprising coverage fact or gap many clients overlook, (4) Reminder or tip relevant to the season (home prep, driving safety, tax time, etc.), (5) CTA — "Call me about your [specific review or question]". Under 500 words total.

Prompt 8 — Social Media Content Pack

Write a 20-post social media content pack for an insurance agent. My lines: [personal / commercial / life / health] Platforms: [Facebook / LinkedIn / Instagram] Tone: [educational / warm / slightly humorous] Compliance: No specific coverage guarantees, no unsolicited advice. Post types: 5 educational tips (one per coverage area) → 3 common coverage myths debunked → 3 life event triggers (buying a home, having a baby, starting a business) → 2 client story posts (no names, no specifics — general situation) → 3 seasonal tips (storm prep, summer travel, holiday safety) → 2 personal/behind-the-scenes → 2 CTA posts (review offer). Each post: Under 200 words, includes a question to drive engagement, avoids compliance landmines.

4. Client Retention

Prompt 9 — Renewal Communication Sequence

Write a renewal communication sequence for auto or home insurance clients. Timeline: 60 days before renewal → 30 days → 14 days → renewal day Compliance: Don't promise specific rates or coverage without underwriting. Tone: Proactive and helpful — not "please don't leave." Touch 1 (60 days out): Heads up + offer to review before renewal. Touch 2 (30 days): Review benefits of their current coverage + easy scheduling. Touch 3 (14 days): Final review opportunity + confirm everything looks right. Touch 4 (renewal): Confirmation + thank you + small value-add (tip, reminder). Each: Subject line + email body under 150 words. These emails should feel like service, not retention marketing.
Pro tip: The 30-day touchpoint is your best retention window. Clients who talk to their agent before renewal churn at half the rate of those who don't.

Prompt 10 — At-Risk Client Recovery

Write communications for clients who are at risk of cancelling or have already received a competitor quote. Situation type: (1) Client says they found a cheaper rate, (2) Client missed a payment and coverage lapsed, (3) Client called to cancel — unclear reason, (4) Client hasn't responded in 12+ months (ghost client risk). For each: Script or email that: Acknowledges their situation without being desperate → Offers genuine value or service reason to stay → Asks the right question to understand what's really happening → Makes staying easy or saves the relationship even if they leave. Don't beg. Do focus on the relationship and value.

5. Business & Niche Development

Prompt 11 — Niche Market Strategy

Help me build a niche strategy for my insurance practice. Lines I sell: [list] My background or knowledge: [industries, life stages, or situations I understand well] Geographic market: [local / statewide / national] Goal: [more referrals / higher premiums / specific industry focus / all] Identify: (1) My top 2–3 niche opportunities (industry, life stage, or situation) based on my background, (2) The specific coverage needs of each niche that I can speak to credibly, (3) Referral partners in each niche (who already serves them), (4) Content topics that would attract each niche organically, (5) How to position myself as "the [niche] insurance specialist" vs. a generalist. Start with 1 niche — depth before breadth.

Prompt 12 — Commercial Lines Prospecting

Write a commercial lines prospecting strategy for [target industry: contractors / restaurants / tech startups / healthcare practices / retail / professional services]. My commercial lines expertise: [what I specialize in] Typical coverage package for this industry: [general — no specifics without underwriting] Common coverage gaps in this industry: [what they often overlook] Referral partners who work with this industry: [accountants / attorneys / industry associations / chambers] Create: (1) Outreach message for business owners in this industry, (2) LinkedIn content idea that demonstrates expertise (without specific advice), (3) 5 questions to ask in a commercial review that uncover coverage gaps, (4) How to partner with associations or groups that serve this industry, (5) The #1 coverage gap in this industry I can lead with to open conversations.
Pro tip: Commercial clients have higher premiums, multi-policy potential, and lower churn than personal lines. One good commercial referral partner can be worth a dozen personal lines prospects.

Prompt 13 — Agency Hiring & Team Communication

Help me communicate more effectively with my agency team or new hires. My role: [agency owner / team leader / senior agent] Team size: [number of agents or staff] Current challenge: [onboarding new agents / motivating the team / communicating processes / client service standards] Write: (1) Job posting for a new insurance agent / customer service rep (highlight culture and growth), (2) 30-60-90 day onboarding outline for a new agent, (3) Weekly team meeting agenda template, (4) Script for coaching a team member who is underperforming, (5) Recognition email for a team win (retention, new business, great client feedback). Keep it human — great agencies have great culture.

Prompt 14 — Google Business Profile Optimization

Help me optimize my Google Business Profile as an insurance agent. My name: [name] | Agency: [name] | Lines: [what I sell] | Location: [city] My specialty or niche: [if any] Reviews I have: [describe what clients say most] Write: (1) Business description (750 chars max — keyword-rich, benefit-led, no compliance red flags), (2) 5 Google Posts (weekly content ideas that drive calls/clicks), (3) Q&A section — 8 questions to self-publish with answers, (4) Review response templates (3 positive, 2 negative), (5) Photo caption templates for staff and office photos. Local visibility on Google often drives more new clients than social media for insurance agents.

Prompt 15 — Annual Business Review

Help me review my insurance agency / book of business this year. Book size: [$premium / number of policies / number of clients] Retention rate: [%] | New business: [$premium / policies] Best referral sources: [list] Top performing lines: [list] Clients I lost (general reasons): [describe] Time I spent on: [sales / service / admin / marketing] Analyze: (1) My real retention rate by line — where am I leaking? (2) My best referral source — how to get more from it, (3) My time allocation problem — what am I doing that isn't building my book? (4) My top 3 priorities for next year (specific), (5) The honest question: what's stopping me from doubling my production in 24 months?
