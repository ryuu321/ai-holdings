ChatGPT Prompts for YouTube Creators

15 free prompts to write better scripts, optimize your titles and thumbnails, plan your content, and grow a channel that makes real money.

The creators growing fastest on YouTube in 2026 aren't working harder — they're using AI to write faster, iterate more, and show up consistently. These 15 prompts cut the busywork so you can focus on what actually matters: creating great videos your audience keeps coming back for.

1. Video Ideation & Strategy

Prompt 1 — Video Idea Generator

I make YouTube videos about [your niche/topic]. My target audience is [describe: age, level, what they're trying to achieve]. My channel's tone is [educational / entertaining / inspirational / documentary-style]. Generate 20 video ideas for my channel. For each: (1) Title (click-worthy but accurate), (2) Core premise in 1 sentence, (3) Why this would perform well (search demand / trending / evergreen / curiosity gap), (4) Suggested video length. Mix: tutorials, opinion pieces, reaction/response videos, list videos, story-based videos, and keyword-driven evergreen content. Flag the 3 with highest viral potential.
Pro tip: Run your top 3 ideas through YouTube's search bar and check autocomplete suggestions. Real search data beats AI guesses every time.

Prompt 2 — Channel Positioning & Niche Strategy

Help me define my YouTube channel positioning. My current content: [describe what you've been making] My unique angle/experience: [what makes you different] Target audience: [who you want to reach] My goal: [subscribers / revenue / brand deals / community] Competing channels I admire: [list 2–3] Analyze: (1) What niche can I own — specific enough to win, broad enough to grow? (2) What content pillars should I build around? (3) Who is my "anti-audience" (who is NOT for me)? (4) What would make someone subscribe after watching 1 video? (5) Suggested channel tagline that communicates my value in under 10 words.

2. Script Writing

Prompt 3 — Full Video Script

Write a YouTube script for a [length: 8–12 minute] video on [topic]. My audience: [describe] My style: [conversational / educational / story-driven / list-based] Goal of this video: [inform / entertain / inspire / drive to CTA] Script structure: Hook (first 30 seconds — stop the click-off) → Context/credibility setup → Main content (3–5 sections with transitions) → Pattern interrupt at 40% and 70% of video → CTA (subscribe or linked offer) → Outro. Include [B-roll suggestions] in brackets. Write naturally — this should sound like I'm talking, not reading.
Pro tip: Write your hook last. Once you know the full content, you know what's actually most compelling to open with.

Prompt 4 — Hook Variations

Write 10 different opening hooks for my YouTube video about [topic]. My audience: [describe] The video's core insight or takeaway: [what they'll learn] Variation types to include: (1) Bold controversial statement, (2) Question that creates curiosity, (3) Story opener — a specific moment, (4) Counter-intuitive claim, (5) Result/outcome statement ("By the end of this video you'll know..."), (6) Pain point opener, (7) Statistics that surprise, (8) "Most people think X, but actually Y", (9) Personal failure story, (10) Direct challenge or dare. Rate each 1–10 for click-retention potential and explain why.

3. Titles, Thumbnails & SEO

Prompt 5 — Title & Thumbnail Optimizer

I'm publishing a YouTube video about [topic]. Core message: [what the video proves or teaches]. My channel size: [subscriber count]. Generate: (1) 10 title options — mix of SEO-optimized (include keyword: "[keyword]") and click-through optimized (curiosity, emotion, outcome). Mark each as "SEO" or "CTR" focus. (2) 5 thumbnail concepts — describe the visual, the text overlay (3–5 words max), and the emotion it triggers. (3) Flag the title+thumbnail combo most likely to get clicked. Explain the psychology.
Pro tip: The thumbnail and title work as a unit. Your thumbnail creates curiosity; your title answers just enough to justify clicking.

Prompt 6 — YouTube SEO Description

Write an SEO-optimized YouTube description for my video: "[video title]" Target keyword: [primary keyword] Secondary keywords: [2–3 related terms] Video content summary: [brief description] Chapters: [list your timestamps and topics] Links to include: [subscribe link, website, social, product] Format: First 2 lines (visible before "show more") must be compelling and keyword-rich → Full description (300+ words) → Chapters → Links → Hashtags (5–8). Don't keyword-stuff — write for humans first, algorithm second.

4. Audience & Community

Prompt 7 — Comment Response Templates

I need templates for responding to different types of YouTube comments. My niche: [niche]. My tone: [warm/direct/humorous]. Write response templates for: (1) Genuine appreciation comment, (2) Question about the video topic, (3) Constructive criticism, (4) Negative or unfair criticism, (5) "Can you make a video about X?" request, (6) Spam or irrelevant comment (to ignore), (7) Comment from another creator, (8) Long thoughtful comment that deserves a real reply. Each template: under 3 sentences, sounds like me (not corporate), leaves them feeling heard. Include how to personalize each.
Pro tip: Reply to the first 20–30 comments in the first hour of posting. Early engagement signals boost the video algorithmically.

Prompt 8 — Community Post Ideas

Generate 20 YouTube Community Post ideas for my [niche] channel. My audience: [describe] Recent video topics: [list 3–5 recent videos] Post types to include: Polls (3), Questions to spark discussion (4), Behind-the-scenes text updates (3), Image/gif post hooks (3), Mini-tips that stand alone (3), Personal story shares (2), Video previews/teasers (2). Each post should be under 150 words. Flag the 5 most likely to generate high comment engagement.

5. Monetization & Business

Prompt 9 — Sponsorship Pitch Email

Write a brand sponsorship pitch email for my YouTube channel. My channel: [name] — [subscriber count], [niche], [average views] Audience demographics: [age, location, interests] Relevant stats: [engagement rate, email list if any, social following] Target brand: [company name and what they sell] Why the fit: [why my audience cares about this brand] What I'm offering: [dedicated video / integration / shorts / email mention] Asking for: $[rate] Write a 200-word email that: Opens with their brand specifically (not a template intro) → Shows I know their product → Presents my audience as their ideal customer → States what I'm proposing clearly → Ends with a low-friction next step.

Prompt 10 — Digital Product Idea Generator

I'm a YouTube creator in [niche] with [subscriber count] subscribers. I want to create a digital product to monetize beyond AdSense. My audience's biggest problem: [describe] What I'm most knowledgeable about: [your expertise] Content types that perform best on my channel: [tutorials / stories / lists / reviews] Price range I'm targeting: [$9–$47 / $47–$197 / $197+] Generate 5 digital product ideas with: Product type, working title, what's inside (3–5 bullet points), pricing rationale, and why my existing audience would buy this. Flag the easiest to create and the highest revenue potential.
Pro tip: Your best-performing videos reveal your audience's biggest wants. Build the product that's a deeper version of your top 3 videos.

6. Growth & Analytics

Prompt 11 — Channel Audit & Growth Plan

Audit my YouTube channel and create a growth plan. Channel info: [niche, subscriber count, avg views per video, subscriber growth rate last 90 days] Top 3 performing videos: [titles and view counts] Worst 3 performing videos: [titles and view counts] What I've tried that didn't work: [describe experiments] My current upload schedule: [frequency] My goal: [X subscribers / X revenue by when] Analyze: (1) What pattern explains my top performers? (2) What should I stop doing? (3) My 3 highest-leverage growth actions in the next 60 days, (4) Upload frequency recommendation given my goal, (5) One thing I'm probably avoiding that would move the needle most.

Prompt 12 — Shorts Strategy

Build a YouTube Shorts strategy for my [niche] channel. Main channel size: [subscribers, avg views] Current Shorts experience: [never done / tried a few / somewhat consistent] Content I have: [existing long-form videos / footage / screen recordings / talking head] Strategy: (1) What Shorts format works best for my niche, (2) How to repurpose existing long-form into Shorts (step-by-step), (3) 10 original Shorts ideas, (4) Optimal posting frequency for Shorts alongside main channel, (5) How to use Shorts to funnel new viewers to my long-form content. Include hook formulas specific to Shorts format.

7. Workflow & Consistency

Prompt 13 — 30-Day Content Calendar

Build a 30-day YouTube content calendar for my [niche] channel. Upload schedule: [X videos/week on main, X Shorts/week] Upcoming events or seasonal tie-ins: [list or "none"] Content pillars: [list 3–4 recurring content themes] Analytics insight: [what's been working lately] Calendar format: For each video — publish date, title, format (tutorial / opinion / story / list), thumbnail concept, target keyword, call to action in video. Balance evergreen content with trending/timely topics. Include 1 "YouTube bait" (high-CTR, algorithm-friendly) video per month.
Pro tip: Batch script and record in blocks. 1 day of scripting + 1 day of filming covers 2–3 weeks of content. Consistency beats burst production.

Prompt 14 — Video Repurposing System

Build a repurposing system for my YouTube videos. My channel: [niche, upload frequency] Platforms I want to reach: [Instagram / TikTok / Twitter/X / LinkedIn / email / podcast / blog] Time I can spend on repurposing per video: [hours] Create a repurposing workflow: For each platform — what to extract from the video, how to adapt it (format, length, tone), and when to post relative to the YouTube publish date. Include: (1) The 20% of repurposing that drives 80% of results, (2) Templates I can reuse for each format, (3) Tools that make this faster. I want a system, not a one-off list.

Prompt 15 — Creator Burnout Prevention

I'm a YouTube creator in [niche] and I'm experiencing [burnout / creative block / motivation dip / consistency struggles]. Context: [how long I've been creating, upload history, what changed recently] What I love about creating: [describe] What drains me most: [list the specific tasks] My audience's relationship with me: [describe — do they depend on a schedule?] Help me: (1) Redesign my content workflow to protect my energy, (2) Identify which tasks I can simplify, batch, or eliminate, (3) Create a sustainable schedule that doesn't require "perfect" motivation, (4) What to tell my audience while I reset, (5) The 1 mindset shift that most long-term creators credit for longevity.
