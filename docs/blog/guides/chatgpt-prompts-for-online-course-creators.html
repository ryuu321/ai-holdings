ChatGPT Prompts for Online Course Creators

15 free prompts to design better courses, write sales pages that convert, launch to your audience, and build a profitable course business — with AI doing the heavy lifting.

Building a successful online course isn't about recording great content — it's about understanding your student's transformation, communicating it clearly, and building the systems to sell and deliver at scale. These 15 prompts help you do all of it faster.

1. Course Design & Validation

Prompt 1 — Course Idea Validation

Help me validate my online course idea. Course idea: [describe your topic and what students would learn] Target student: [who is this for — specific role, situation, goal] The transformation I'm promising: [before state → after state] How I currently know there's demand: [email list / social following / comments / sales conversations] Price I'm considering: $[amount] Validate this idea: (1) How specific is the transformation — is it concrete enough that students can judge if they achieved it? (2) Who is my real competitor — what are students doing today instead of my course? (3) What would make a student choose my course over a YouTube playlist or a book? (4) The most common reason this type of course fails — how do I avoid it? (5) What's the minimum evidence I need before building the full course?
Pro tip: The fastest validation is preselling before you build. 10 paying students for a "founding member" beta proves demand better than 1,000 email subscribers who never buy.

Prompt 2 — Course Curriculum Builder

Build a curriculum for my online course. Topic: [what the course teaches] Target student: [who they are and what they already know] The core transformation: [what changes from module 1 to the end] Length target: [4 / 6 / 8 / 12 weeks or "self-paced"] Deliverable: [video lessons / workbooks / templates / coaching calls / community] Structure: (1) Course title and subtitle, (2) Module-by-module breakdown (5–8 modules) with: module title → learning objective (what student can DO after) → 3–5 lesson topics per module, (3) The logical progression (why this order matters), (4) Where students most commonly get stuck — and how to design around it, (5) Quick win in Module 1 (what students accomplish in the first lesson that creates momentum).

2. Sales Page & Marketing

Prompt 3 — Sales Page Writer

Write a sales page for my online course. Course: [name] Target student: [describe in detail — their situation, struggle, desire] The transformation: [before → after, as specific as possible] Price: $[amount] Format: [video / self-paced / live cohort] Length: [hours / weeks] Proof: [testimonials / beta results / your credentials] Objections they have: [list 3–5] Sales page sections: (1) Headline + sub-headline (outcome-focused, not "learn X"), (2) Opening section — who this is for (make them say "that's me"), (3) "Imagine if..." future-pacing section, (4) Problem statement (agitate without being manipulative), (5) Solution intro (the course + your unique approach), (6) What's inside (curriculum overview), (7) Who this is for / who it's not for, (8) Proof section (testimonials + case studies), (9) FAQ (5–7 objections), (10) Guarantee, (11) CTA section. No hype. Real specifics only.
Pro tip: The "who this is NOT for" section makes the "who this IS for" section more credible. Specificity builds trust. Vague sales pages get ignored.

Prompt 4 — Launch Email Sequence

Write a course launch email sequence. Course: [name] | Price: $[amount] | Cart open: [X days] Audience: [email list size, what they already know about you] Launch angle: [what makes this course timely or compelling right now] Objections to address: [cost / time / "I could find this free" / doubt they can do it] Sequence (send over 7–10 days): Email 1 (launch day): Open cart — lead with the transformation, not features. Email 2 (+2 days): Social proof / student story (real or beta result). Email 3 (+4 days): Objection handler — address the #1 reason they're not buying yet. Email 4 (+6 days): FAQ / "what's actually inside" — for people who need more detail. Email 5 (penultimate day): Urgency — why deciding now matters (closing deadline or bonus). Email 6 (final hours): Last chance — honest, not desperate. Each email: Subject line + 200–300 words.

3. Student Experience

Prompt 5 — Welcome & Onboarding Sequence

Write a student welcome and onboarding sequence for my course. Course: [name] | Format: [self-paced / live cohort / hybrid] New student's likely mindset: [excited but unsure where to start / nervous / motivated but overwhelmed] Quick win I want them to get in the first session: [describe] Community I have (if any): [Facebook / Slack / Circle / Discord / none] Create: (1) Purchase confirmation email (warm, congratulations, what happens next), (2) Welcome email with start instructions (Day 1 — clear, simple, enthusiastic), (3) Day 3 check-in ("how are you doing?" — identify early strugglers), (4) Day 7 milestone email (celebrate their progress on Module 1), (5) Community welcome post template (for students to introduce themselves). Make students feel like they made the right decision the moment they enroll.

Prompt 6 — Re-Engagement Message for Inactive Students

Write messages to re-engage students who've stopped progressing. Course: [name] | Student drop-off point: [where most people stop — module X] Common reasons students stop: [too busy / got overwhelmed / hit a technical block / lost motivation] What I want them to do: [resume the course / book a coaching call / post in community] Write 3 re-engagement messages for different situations: (1) General inactivity (7+ days, no specific reason) — warm check-in, (2) After a hard module — "this is the toughest part, you're not alone", (3) Before the cohort ends / access deadline — urgency without guilt. Each: Under 150 words. Empathetic, not shaming. The goal is to help them succeed, not protect your completion rate.
Pro tip: The Module 1 → Module 2 dropout is usually a lack of quick wins, not a content problem. Audit your first module for a clear, achievable milestone students can complete and celebrate.

4. Audience Building

Prompt 7 — Lead Magnet Creator

Help me create a lead magnet to build my email list for my upcoming course. Course topic: [describe] Target audience: [who I want to attract] Platform I'll promote on: [Instagram / YouTube / LinkedIn / Pinterest / TikTok] My goal: [email list growth → course sales] Suggest 5 lead magnet ideas: For each — title, format (checklist / template / mini-course / quiz / challenge / workbook), what's inside (3 bullets), and why this would attract my ideal course student. Then write the full copy for my top choice: opt-in page headline → sub-headline → 4 bullet points of what they'll learn → CTA button text.

Prompt 8 — Free Content Strategy

Build a free content strategy to grow my audience and prime them to buy my course. Course topic: [what I teach] | Target student: [describe] Current audience: [platform, size, engagement level] Posting frequency: [X times/week] Course launch date: [target date or "in 90 days"] Content strategy: (1) 3 content pillars that establish credibility AND attract my ideal buyer, (2) 20 post/video ideas across those pillars, (3) The content type that best demonstrates the course transformation (not just teaches), (4) How to weave in social proof and course preview naturally — without making every post a pitch, (5) Content cadence for the 30 days before launch (when to start warming the audience).

5. Pricing & Revenue

Prompt 9 — Pricing Strategy

Help me decide the right price for my online course. Course topic: [describe] Target student: [who they are and what outcome they get] Course format: [self-paced videos / live cohort / done-with-you / all-access] Comparable courses in the market: [describe or "I don't know"] My audience size: [email list, social following] Revenue goal from first launch: $[amount] My credibility / proof: [describe] Analyze: (1) The value equation — what is the outcome worth to the student in time or money saved / earned? (2) Price anchors in the market — what's the low, mid, and high end? (3) What my price signals about my positioning, (4) Launch pricing vs. evergreen pricing strategy, (5) Payment plan math — how do payment plans affect my revenue and cash flow? Recommend a price with rationale.
Pro tip: Most course creators underprice by 30–50%. If your course teaches someone to make $5K/month, charging $97 is irrational. Price in proportion to the transformation delivered.

Prompt 10 — Passive Income Scaling Plan

Build a passive income scaling plan for my course business. Current course: [describe] | Price: $[amount] | Current students: [number] Monthly revenue: $[amount] | Goal: $[amount]/month Current audience: [size and platforms] Time I want to invest going forward: [hours/week — I want more passive income] Plan: (1) Evergreen funnel strategy — how to sell without live launches, (2) Lead generation system that runs automatically (which platform and content type), (3) Email automation sequence post-opt-in (how long, what it says), (4) Conversion rate benchmarks — what's realistic for my audience type, (5) When to build a second product and what it should be. Show me the math: at [X] monthly visitors / [Y]% email opt-in / [Z]% email-to-sale, my monthly revenue becomes $[amount].

6. Partnerships & Affiliates

Prompt 11 — Affiliate Partner Outreach

Write outreach messages to recruit affiliate partners for my course. Course: [name] | Price: $[amount] | Commission: [%] Target affiliates: [complementary course creators / newsletter owners / podcast hosts / YouTube creators in adjacent niches] My audience size: [for credibility reference] Results from students: [social proof to share] Write: (1) Email to a newsletter owner (150 words — specific, show why their audience would love this), (2) DM to a podcast host (under 100 words — pitch to be a guest + mention affiliate potential), (3) Email to a course creator in a complementary niche (peer-to-peer, collaborative tone), (4) Follow-up if no response in 2 weeks. Each: leads with value to their audience, not commission percentage.

Prompt 12 — Joint Venture Webinar Script

Write a joint venture webinar script for my course launch. Course: [name] | Price: $[amount] JV partner: [who they are and their audience] Webinar topic: [what you'll teach in 45–60 minutes that leads to the course pitch] My credibility: [credentials, results, proof] Webinar offer: [early bird price / bonus / limited seats] Script structure: (1) Partner introduction of you (100 words — they read this), (2) Your credibility opener (5 minutes), (3) Core content (30–40 minutes — teach real value, don't hold back), (4) Transition to offer (5 minutes — "if you want to go deeper..."), (5) Offer presentation (10 minutes — price, what's included, guarantee, CTA), (6) Live Q&A handling — 5 common questions with suggested answers. Keep the pitch conversational — this is a recommendation from a trusted friend, not a sales event.

7. Operations & Growth

Prompt 13 — Student Testimonial System

Build a testimonial collection system for my course. My course: [name] | Format: [cohort / self-paced] Best time to ask for testimonials: [after Module X / 30 days in / at course completion] What I need: [written testimonials / video testimonials / case studies / all] Create: (1) The trigger moment — exactly when and how to ask, (2) Email request for written testimonial (includes 5 guided questions that produce specific, compelling quotes), (3) Video testimonial request (what to say, how to make recording easy), (4) How to turn a great story into a case study (structure + permission request), (5) Where to place testimonials for maximum impact (sales page placement strategy). The goal: testimonials that make future students say "that's exactly my situation."

Prompt 14 — Course Iteration Plan

Help me improve my existing course based on student feedback. Course: [name] | Students who've completed: [number] Student completion rate: [%] Most common positive feedback: [what they love] Most common complaints or struggles: [what's not working] Drop-off point: [where most students stop] What I want to improve: [describe] Improvement plan: (1) Quick wins — changes I can make immediately without rebuilding, (2) The module or section that most needs a redesign and why, (3) What additional material or support would improve completion, (4) How to update students about changes (and make them feel good about it), (5) What to test in the next cohort before committing to a full rebuild. Fix the 20% causing 80% of the friction.

Prompt 15 — Annual Course Business Review

Help me review my online course business this year. Revenue: $[amount] | Students enrolled: [number] | Completion rate: [%] Launches: [number, type, results] Best-performing marketing channel: [describe] What students said they loved most: [themes] Biggest challenge this year: [describe] Where I am vs. my goal: [honest gap] Analyze: (1) Revenue per student — is this trending in the right direction? (2) Where is student success happening and why — can I create more of it? (3) My most effective marketing action this year — am I doing enough of it? (4) The 1 thing I should stop doing entirely, (5) My 3 priorities for next year and what achieving them requires.
