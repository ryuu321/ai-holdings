ChatGPT Prompts for Financial Advisors

15 free prompts to write better client communications, explain complex concepts in plain language, grow your practice, and save hours on admin every week.

Financial advisors spend enormous amounts of time writing — market commentaries, client emails, educational content, meeting prep — and most of it doesn't require financial expertise, just the ability to communicate clearly. These 15 prompts handle the writing so your expertise can focus where it matters most.

Important: These prompts assist with communication and practice management — not with investment recommendations or regulated financial advice. Never input client account details, portfolio data, or personally identifiable financial information into AI tools. Review all AI-generated content for accuracy and compliance with your firm's policies and applicable regulations before use.

1. Client Communication

Prompt 1 — Market Commentary Email

Write a client market commentary email for [month/quarter]. Key market events to address: [list 3–5: rate decisions, equity performance, sector moves, economic data] Client sentiment risk: [are clients nervous about X? Address it] Your perspective: [what your view is — calm, cautious, opportunistic] Action: [no change / taking an opportunity / adjusting positioning — keep vague for general audience] Tone: [calm and grounding / educational / direct] Write: Subject line → Opening (acknowledge what clients may be feeling) → Brief market summary (avoid jargon) → Your perspective (what this means for long-term investors) → What we're doing / not doing and why → Reassuring close with next step (call me if you want to talk). Under 400 words. No specific return predictions. No investment advice language that needs compliance review beyond standard disclaimers.
Pro tip: The best market commentaries acknowledge what clients are feeling before explaining what's happening. Validation first, information second.

Prompt 2 — Financial Concept Explainer

Explain [financial concept] to a client in plain language. Concept: [sequence of returns risk / dollar cost averaging / rebalancing / asset allocation / tax-loss harvesting / RMDs / Roth conversion ladder / Monte Carlo simulation / sequence of returns / inflation impact on retirement / etc.] Client profile: [age range, investment sophistication level, what they're worried about] Write a 200-word explanation that: Uses one concrete analogy (not the typical textbook one) → Avoids jargon or immediately defines it → Connects the concept to this client's situation → Ends with the key takeaway in one sentence. This will be copied into a client email — it must be accurate and readable, not textbook.

Prompt 3 — Meeting Prep Brief

Help me prepare for an annual review meeting with a client. Client profile (general, no specific financial data): [age bracket, life stage, general goals — e.g., "late 50s couple, 10 years from retirement, concerned about healthcare costs"] Topics I need to cover: [list 4–6] Client's likely concerns based on recent events: [describe — market volatility, life event, news item] Decisions they may need to make: [list] Goal for this meeting: [reassurance / strategic update / rebalancing conversation / estate planning intro] Create: (1) Meeting agenda (60–minute structure), (2) Questions to open each topic that get clients talking (not lecturing them), (3) How to address anticipated concerns, (4) Action items framework — what I'll do, what they'll do, and by when. This is for my prep — not for the client.
Pro tip: The advisor who asks great questions builds deeper trust than the one who gives great answers. Prepare your questions as carefully as your talking points.

2. Practice Marketing

Prompt 4 — LinkedIn Content Plan

Build a 30-day LinkedIn content plan for a financial advisor. My specialty: [retirement planning / wealth management / business owners / high-net-worth / young professionals / women in finance — choose your niche] My audience on LinkedIn: [describe: who I'm trying to reach] Compliance constraint: [no specific investment recommendations, no performance claims] Goal: [brand awareness / referral partner relationships / direct client inquiries] 30 content ideas: For each — format (article / short post / poll / video script), topic/angle, hook (opening line), and CTA (if appropriate). Mix: educational financial concepts → personal stories about working with clients (anonymized) → opinion pieces on industry trends → community questions → case studies (general, no client data). Ratio: 90% value, 10% business development.

Prompt 5 — Email Newsletter Template

Write a monthly client newsletter template for financial advisors. My practice specialty: [describe] Topics I cover regularly: [financial planning concepts / market context / life events and money / retirement / etc.] Client audience: [describe your typical client] Compliance note: No specific investment advice or return predictions. Newsletter sections: (1) "From my desk" — brief personal note (100 words, relationship-building, not a market update), (2) Financial education piece — one concept explained simply (200 words), (3) "Worth reading" — 2–3 resources I'm recommending (books, articles, tools), (4) "Think about this" — a question for clients to reflect on (relates to financial planning), (5) Office news / upcoming availability. This newsletter should feel like it comes from a trusted advisor, not a financial firm.

Prompt 6 — Referral Partner Outreach

Write outreach emails to build referral relationships with [CPAs / estate attorneys / business brokers / divorce attorneys / HR directors]. My practice: [specialty, client type, credentials] What I offer their clients: [describe your value to their referrals] What I offer them: [how I make their work easier / referrals back / joint content] Write: (1) Cold email to a CPA (150 words — reference their firm specifically, propose a specific next step), (2) LinkedIn message version (under 200 words), (3) Follow-up after no response (2 weeks — shorter, different angle). Each message must feel personal, not templated. The goal is a coffee meeting or intro call, not a sales pitch.
Pro tip: CPAs and estate attorneys get dozens of advisor pitches. Reference a specific client scenario where your work and theirs would overlap. Specificity gets meetings; generality gets ignored.

3. Client Lifecycle

Prompt 7 — New Client Welcome Package

Write a welcome package for new financial advisory clients. My practice: [name, specialty] What new clients are often feeling: [anxious about the process / excited / uncertain if they made the right choice] My onboarding process: [describe the first 90 days] What makes working with me different: [your approach] Create: (1) Welcome letter (200 words — warm, reassuring, sets expectations), (2) "What to expect in your first 90 days" document (step-by-step process), (3) FAQ section (8 questions new clients typically ask — with answers), (4) "Things that would help us help you" list — documents and information to gather. Make them feel: organized, in good hands, and clear on next steps.

Prompt 8 — Client Life Event Follow-Up

Write follow-up communications for clients going through major life events. Write 6 email templates for: (1) Recent marriage — congratulations + planning conversation starter, (2) New baby / growing family — warm note + "when you're ready to talk" planning prompt, (3) Job change or promotion — acknowledgment + compensation/benefits planning nudge, (4) Recent divorce — sensitive outreach (don't assume they want to talk immediately), (5) Loss of a spouse — condolences (don't pitch anything — just be human), (6) Upcoming retirement within 1 year — proactive planning invitation. Each email: Under 150 words, feels human not transactional, ends with a gentle (not pushy) next step. Life event emails are the highest-trust moments in the advisor relationship — treat them accordingly.

4. Education & Content

Prompt 9 — Client Workshop / Webinar Outline

Build a client educational workshop outline on [topic: retirement income planning / tax-efficient investing / estate planning basics / women and wealth / planning for business owners / market volatility and your portfolio]. Audience: [describe your typical client] Format: [in-person seminar / virtual webinar / intimate dinner event] Length: [45 / 60 / 90 minutes] Goal: [educate existing clients / attract new prospects / both] Outline: (1) Opening hook (why this topic matters right now), (2) The 3–4 core concepts with examples, (3) Common mistakes people make, (4) Q&A facilitation guide (questions to prompt discussion), (5) Takeaway action for attendees, (6) Close and CTA (next step with you). Include: what materials to prepare and how to handle compliance review of presentation content.

Prompt 10 — Frequently Asked Question Bank

Build a FAQ bank for my financial advisory practice website and client emails. My specialty: [describe] Client life stage focus: [pre-retirees / retirees / business owners / young professionals] Topics clients ask about most: [list 5–8] Write 20 FAQs with plain-language answers. Topics to cover: How I work and who I work with → What to expect from our relationship → How I'm compensated → How I approach investment decisions (general) → What to bring to our first meeting → Common financial planning questions in my specialty area. Each answer: Under 150 words, accurate, no specific investment advice, and written for someone who doesn't speak finance.
Pro tip: FAQs on your website aren't just for answering questions — they pre-qualify clients and build trust before the first call. Answer the questions clients are afraid to ask.

5. Operations & Growth

Prompt 11 — Practice Niche Positioning

Help me define and communicate my advisory practice niche. My background and expertise: [credentials, experience, specialties] Clients I do my best work with: [describe in detail — profession, life stage, financial situation, values] Clients that create friction or aren't a fit: [be honest] Market I'm in: [location or "virtual"] My goal: [more referrals / higher-net-worth clients / specific industry focus / all] Define: (1) My ideal client profile (specific — not "anyone who needs financial help"), (2) My unique value proposition in 2 sentences, (3) My positioning statement (who I am, who I serve, what I do differently), (4) How to communicate this on LinkedIn, website, and in referral conversations, (5) The type of referral partner who most naturally sends me ideal clients.

Prompt 12 — Prospect Follow-Up Sequence

Write a follow-up sequence for prospects who attended a seminar / downloaded a resource / came through a referral but haven't scheduled yet. My practice: [specialty, typical client] How they first connected with me: [event / referral / download / website] Timeline since contact: [just connected / 1 week / 2+ weeks] Tone: [warm and helpful — not salesy or pushy] Write 3 follow-up touches: Touch 1 (day 2–3 after initial contact): Value-add email — share a relevant resource or insight. Touch 2 (1 week later): Light check-in — personalized reference to why they reached out. Touch 3 (2 weeks later): "Last touch" — low-pressure close with easy next step. Each: under 150 words, sounds human. Compliant: no investment claims or promises.

6. Difficult Conversations

Prompt 13 — Difficult Client Situation Scripts

Help me prepare for difficult conversations with clients. Write scripts for: (1) Client wants to make an emotional investment decision (market panic, hot stock) — how to slow them down without dismissing them, (2) Client who hasn't followed through on agreed planning actions — how to address without guilt-tripping, (3) Raising fees — how to communicate a fee increase professionally and confidently, (4) Client who is unhappy with performance — how to acknowledge concern, reframe context, and preserve the relationship, (5) Ending the relationship with a client who isn't a fit — professional, kind, and clear. For each: Opening framing → Key message → How to handle pushback → Resolution/next step. These conversations protect both the client and my practice.
Pro tip: Clients who feel heard during difficult conversations become your strongest advocates. Lead with acknowledgment before explanation.

Prompt 14 — Compliance-Aware Email Review Checklist

Help me build a pre-send checklist for client-facing emails and content. My registration type: [RIA / broker-dealer / dually registered] Common compliance concerns for my firm: [no performance guarantees / no specific recommendations / required disclosures / record-keeping / no testimonials without approval] Build a 10-point pre-send review checklist I can run through before any client communication goes out. Include: (1) Compliance red flags to watch for, (2) Language that often triggers compliance review, (3) Required disclosures for different content types, (4) How to phrase forward-looking statements safely, (5) When to loop in compliance before sending. Note: This checklist is a starting framework — consult your compliance officer for firm-specific requirements.

Prompt 15 — Annual Practice Review

Help me review my financial advisory practice this year. AUM: $[amount] | Client count: [number] | Revenue: $[amount] Client retention rate: [%] | New clients this year: [number] | Lost clients: [number + general reason] Best client relationships: [what makes them work] Biggest practice challenge: [describe] Where I am vs. my 3-year vision: [honest assessment] Analyze: (1) My most profitable service or client segment — can I serve more of them? (2) Where am I losing clients — and is there a fixable pattern? (3) The referral source that drives my best clients — how to double down, (4) My top 3 practice priorities for next year, (5) The honest answer: what am I avoiding that would most accelerate my practice growth?
