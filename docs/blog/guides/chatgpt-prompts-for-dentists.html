ChatGPT Prompts for Dentists

15 free prompts for dental practices. Write better patient communications, handle reviews, market your practice, and improve patient retention — without extra admin time.

A dental practice runs on clinical excellence and patient relationships — but it grows on communication. The emails you send, the reviews you respond to, the content you publish, and the way you explain treatment plans all determine whether patients stay, refer, and accept care. These 15 prompts help you get the words right, faster.

Important: Never input patient names, PHI, or identifiable health information into AI tools. All patient communication must comply with HIPAA and your state dental board's requirements. Review AI-generated content for clinical accuracy before use.

1. Patient Communication

Prompt 1 — Treatment Plan Explanation Email

Write a patient email explaining a dental treatment plan. Treatment recommended: [describe generally — e.g., "crown and root canal on a molar" / "three-visit Invisalign consult series" / "full-mouth restoration"] Why it's needed: [clinical rationale in patient-friendly language] What happens if they wait or decline: [general consequences without scare tactics] Financial options: [payment plan / financing / insurance coverage note] Next step: [schedule the procedure / consultation] Write a warm, clear 200-word email that: Explains the treatment in plain language (no jargon) → Frames it around their comfort and long-term health → Addresses the "why now" without pressure → Offers to answer questions. Sign it from the doctor. This email should reduce treatment decline by making patients feel informed, not sold.
Pro tip: Patients who understand WHY a treatment is needed accept it at 2–3x the rate of those who just get a list of procedures. Your email is case presentation continued.

Prompt 2 — Reactivation Campaign Email

Write a patient reactivation email for patients who haven't been in for [12 / 18 / 24+] months. Practice: [name] | Location: [city] | Specialty focus: [general / cosmetic / family / pediatric] Tone: [warm and non-judgmental — no guilt about being overdue] Offer (if any): [no offer / complimentary exam / new patient special for lapsed patients] Email: Subject line (curiosity or care-focused — not "You're overdue for your cleaning") → Warm opening → Brief "we've been thinking about you" message → Easy scheduling CTA → No lecture about their dental hygiene. People avoid the dentist when they feel judged. This email should remove that barrier.

Prompt 3 — New Patient Welcome Email

Write a new patient welcome email for a dental practice. Practice: [name] | Dentist: [first name] | Type: [general / family / cosmetic / pediatric] Location and parking: [brief note] What to bring: [ID, insurance, completed forms if applicable] New patient experience: [what makes your practice different — technology, comfort, team] Forms: [link if applicable] Email: Warm welcome → Brief intro to what they'll experience → What to bring → Logistics → "We're looking forward to meeting you" close. Under 200 words. This email is your first impression beyond the phone call. It should make them feel chosen and welcome, not processed.
Pro tip: New patients who feel welcomed before their first visit show up 30% more reliably and convert to regular patients at higher rates. The welcome email matters more than most practices realize.

2. Online Reputation

Prompt 4 — Google Review Response Templates

Write Google review response templates for a dental practice. Practice: [name] | Dentist(s): [first name(s)] | Tone: [warm / professional] HIPAA note: Do NOT confirm or reference any patient's treatment, visit, or health information in responses. Write response templates for: (1) 5-star review — mentions specific doctor or staff member, (2) 5-star review — general positive experience, (3) 4-star review — liked the visit but mentioned one issue, (4) 3-star review — neutral, unclear concern, (5) 1-star review — valid concern about wait time, (6) 1-star review — appears unfair or inaccurate, (7) Review that mentions anxiety or fear — especially empathetic response, (8) Out-of-office hours generic response note. Each: Acknowledges the reviewer, doesn't confirm they're a patient, keeps the tone warm, and invites follow-up privately if there's a concern.

Prompt 5 — Review Request Email

Write a post-appointment email requesting a Google review. Practice: [name] | Appointment type: [cleaning / new patient / post-treatment follow-up] Google review link: [URL placeholder] Compliance: Cannot ask for only positive reviews or incentivize reviews. Email: Subject line (care-focused, not "Leave us a review") → Brief "how was your visit?" opening → Honest ask to share their experience (no mention of "5 stars") → Link to Google review → Thank them for choosing your practice. Under 100 words. This email should arrive 24–48 hours after their appointment when the experience is fresh. HIPAA note: Reference "your recent visit" not the specific treatment.

3. Practice Marketing

Prompt 6 — Social Media Content Plan

Build a 30-day social media content plan for my dental practice. Practice type: [general / cosmetic / orthodontics / pediatric / all-on-4 implants] Platforms: [Instagram / Facebook / TikTok] Posting frequency: [X times/week] HIPAA note: No patient photos without signed release. No clinical information about specific patients. Goal: [brand awareness / new patient acquisition / patient education] 30 content ideas: For each — format (photo / reel / infographic / carousel / post), topic/angle, caption hook, hashtags. Mix: oral health tips, before-after smile content (consented), team spotlights, "why we love dentistry" moments, dental myth-busting, patient FAQ answers, community involvement, and special offers. Ratio: 80% education/relationship, 20% promotional.
Pro tip: Before-and-after smile content consistently outperforms all other dental content types — but only with explicit written patient consent and proper release forms. Invest in getting these right.

Prompt 7 — Google Ads & Local SEO Keywords

Help me build a local SEO and Google Ads keyword strategy for my dental practice. Practice: [name] | Location: [city, neighborhood] | Specialty: [general / cosmetic / implants / orthodontics / pediatric / emergency] My top services: [list 3–5] Nearby competition: [heavy / moderate / light] Budget for Google Ads: $[monthly] Generate: (1) 20 local SEO keyword targets (location + service phrases), (2) 15 Google Ads keywords organized by service (exact/phrase match), (3) 10 negative keywords to exclude irrelevant traffic, (4) 3 ad headline suggestions for my primary service, (5) Google Business Profile categories and description tips. Mark which keywords have emergency/urgent intent (higher value for same-day appointment slots).

4. Patient Education

Prompt 8 — Patient Education Content

Write patient education content on [dental topic: gum disease / teeth whitening / Invisalign vs. braces / dental implants vs. bridges / sleep apnea / teeth grinding / cavities and sugar / children's dental habits / dental anxiety]. Audience: [general adult patients / parents of young children / adults 50+] Format: [email newsletter / social media post / website FAQ / waiting room handout] Reading level: [accessible — no dental school vocabulary] Write a 200-word piece that: Explains the topic clearly → Addresses a common misconception → Gives one actionable takeaway → Invites them to ask their dentist about it (soft CTA). Have your dental team review for clinical accuracy before publishing.

Prompt 9 — Post-Procedure Care Instructions

Write patient post-procedure care instructions for [procedure: extraction / root canal / crown / whitening / Invisalign start / implant / filling]. Tone: [clear and reassuring — not clinical or alarming] Format: [email / printed handout / both] Include: What's normal to experience → What to do and not do in the first 24–48 hours → What to eat and avoid → When to call the office (symptoms that need attention) → Follow-up appointment reminder. Under 300 words. Write for a patient who is still a little numb or anxious — clarity and reassurance matter more than completeness. Have your clinical team review for accuracy before use.
Pro tip: Send post-procedure instructions by email within 1 hour of the appointment. Patients forget verbal instructions — a written version reduces "is this normal?" calls significantly.

5. Team & Operations

Prompt 10 — Staff Job Posting

Write a job posting for a dental office position. Role: [dental hygienist / dental assistant / front desk coordinator / office manager / treatment coordinator] Practice: [name] | Type: [general / specialty] | Location: [city] Hours: [full-time / part-time, days/week] Pay: $[range] + [benefits if any] What makes working here great: [culture, technology, growth, team] Requirements: [experience, certifications, software knowledge] Write a posting that: Leads with what makes us a great place to work → Describes the role honestly → Lists real requirements (not a wish list) → Makes candidates want to apply. Tone: professional but human — not a corporate form. The best candidates have options — write for them, not for the applicant tracking system.

Prompt 11 — Team Meeting Agenda

Write a monthly dental team meeting agenda. Practice type: [general / specialty] Team: [hygienists / assistants / front desk / all staff] Meeting length: [30 / 45 / 60 minutes] Current focus areas: [patient experience / production goals / new technology / team culture / scheduling efficiency] Any issues to address: [general — no patient-specific information] Agenda: (1) Wins from last month (celebrate first), (2) Metrics review (production, collections, new patient count — general), (3) Focus topic of the month (deep dive), (4) Process or protocol reminder, (5) Team input / open floor, (6) Action items with owners. Include: meeting facilitator notes and how to run the "wins" section to start with energy.

6. Special Services & Promotions

Prompt 12 — Cosmetic Dentistry Landing Page

Write a landing page for my cosmetic dentistry services. Services offered: [teeth whitening / veneers / Invisalign / smile makeovers / bonding] Dentist credentials: [relevant training or specialty certifications] Location: [city] Ideal patient: [self-conscious about their smile / upcoming event / always wanted to change their teeth] Before-after imagery: [available or not] Page sections: (1) Hero headline (benefit-focused — the smile they want, not the service), (2) "You deserve to love your smile" section (emotional connection), (3) Services overview (brief — 3–4 with links), (4) Why choose us (credentials, technology, comfort), (5) How it works (3-step process), (6) CTA — free consultation. Write for the patient who Googles "improve my smile [city]" not for your dental colleagues.
Pro tip: Cosmetic patients are buying confidence and how they feel in photos, not dental services. Lead with the outcome, not the procedure.

Prompt 13 — New Resident Welcome Campaign

Write a new resident marketing campaign for my dental practice. Practice: [name] | Location: [city] | Offer: [free exam / new patient discount / no-cost X-rays / "welcome to [city]" package] Target: New residents in [neighborhood / zip codes] Campaign assets: (1) Direct mail postcard copy (100 words — benefit-led, specific offer, easy CTA), (2) Email version for any new resident leads (150 words), (3) Social media post announcing the new patient offer (100 words + hashtags), (4) Google Ads headline options (3 variations). All must clearly state the offer, any terms, and how to claim it. Make them feel genuinely welcomed to the neighborhood.

Prompt 14 — Dental Blog Post

Write a blog post for my dental practice website. Topic: [choosing the right toothbrush / how often to floss / signs you need a root canal / how to manage dental anxiety / dental care during pregnancy / Invisalign vs. braces — pros and cons] Audience: [general adult patients in [city]] SEO target keyword: ["dentist [city]" / "teeth whitening [city]" / "dental anxiety tips" — choose relevant] Goal: [rank locally / educate patients / build trust] Write a 500-word post: Hook (why this matters right now) → 4–5 well-organized sections → Clear, actionable advice → "Talk to your dentist" CTA at the end. No jargon. Accurate information is essential — have your team review before publishing.

Prompt 15 — Annual Practice Review

Help me review my dental practice this year. Production: $[amount] | Collections: $[amount] | New patients: [number] | Retention: [%] Hygiene recall rate: [%] | Case acceptance rate: [%] Best marketing source: [where new patients came from] Staff wins and challenges: [describe generally] What I wanted to achieve this year vs. what happened: [honest gap] Analyze: (1) Where is production leaking — recall, case acceptance, or new patients? (2) My strongest patient acquisition channel — can I invest more here? (3) The #1 thing my patients love about our practice (from reviews) — are we doing enough of it? (4) My top 3 priorities for next year with specific targets, (5) The honest answer: what am I tolerating that's holding the practice back?
