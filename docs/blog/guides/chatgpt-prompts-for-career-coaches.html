ChatGPT Prompts for Career Coaches

15 free prompts to attract more clients, deliver better sessions, and build a thriving career coaching practice with AI.

Career coaches are in the business of transformation — but they still need to write emails, create content, design session frameworks, and run a practice. These 15 prompts handle the writing so your energy goes into the coaching conversations that actually change careers.

1. Client Attraction

Prompt 1 — LinkedIn Content Plan for Career Coaches

Build a 30-day LinkedIn content plan for a career coach. My specialty: [job search coaching / executive coaching / career change / interview prep / salary negotiation / leadership development / specific industry] My ideal client: [describe — mid-career professional / recent grad / tech workers / women returning to work / laid-off professionals] My content tone: [direct and data-driven / warm and encouraging / thought-provoking / practical and tactical] Goal: [attract coaching clients / establish authority / referrals from HR leaders] 30 post ideas: For each — format (post / carousel / video script / poll), topic/angle, hook sentence. Content mix: job search myth-busting → career advice based on your experience → behind-the-scenes of what coaching actually looks like → "what I tell my clients about X" → data or research on the job market → client success themes (no names) → industry trend commentary. 80% value, 20% soft practice-building.
Pro tip: Career coaches who share specific, contrarian career advice — not just "here are 5 resume tips" — attract the best clients. Lead with what you actually believe, not what is safe.

Prompt 2 — Discovery Call Script

Write a discovery call script for a career coaching consultation. My coaching specialty: [describe] My packages: [describe — what is included, length, price range] Typical client goal: [land a new job / get promoted / change careers / negotiate better salary / develop leadership] Common objections: [can not afford it / not sure if I need coaching / not ready yet / already tried things on my own] Write: (1) How to open the call (make them feel heard from the first minute — not a sales call), (2) 8 discovery questions that help you understand their situation and goals, (3) How to present your coaching approach without it feeling like a pitch, (4) How to address each common objection honestly (not manipulation — if coaching is not right for them, say so), (5) How to close toward a decision without pressure, (6) What to do if they say "I need to think about it." Discovery calls are conversations, not pitches — the ones that feel like conversations close more.

Prompt 3 — Email Nurture Sequence for Leads

Write a 5-email nurture sequence for people who downloaded my free resource but have not booked a call. My free resource: [resume template / LinkedIn guide / salary negotiation cheat sheet / career clarity worksheet / job search tracker] Who downloaded it: [job seekers / career changers / people who just got laid off / people unhappy in their current role] My coaching specialty: [describe] Goal: [get them to book a discovery call] 5-email sequence: Email 1 (immediately): deliver the resource + one quick win they can use today → Email 2 (day 3): the biggest mistake people in their situation make → Email 3 (day 6): a client success story (general — no names) that mirrors their situation → Email 4 (day 10): the thing they are probably avoiding that is keeping them stuck → Email 5 (day 14): soft invitation to book a call — honest about what coaching does and does not do. Each email under 250 words, sounds like a person, not a funnel.

2. Client Experience

Prompt 4 — Career Clarity Session Framework

Design a structured career clarity session framework for clients who do not know what they want next. Client profile: [professional who has been doing the same thing for years / recent career disruption / high achiever who is burnt out / someone who has never reflected on career goals before] Session length: [60 / 90 minutes] Goal of the session: [surface what they actually want vs. what they think they should want, identify patterns and values, create a starting direction] Create: (1) Pre-session questions to send the client (5 reflection prompts they complete before we meet), (2) Session structure (phases and timing), (3) The 10 most powerful career clarity questions — with why each one works, (4) How to handle "I do not know" (the most common answer — and how to get past it without leading them), (5) Post-session homework that sustains the clarity, (6) How to capture what emerged in a one-page session summary the client can reference. Career clarity sessions are different from goal-setting sessions — the goal is to surface, not to solve.

Prompt 5 — Resume and LinkedIn Profile Review Template

Build a structured framework for reviewing a client's resume and LinkedIn profile. Client type: [early career / mid-career professional / executive / career changer / returning to workforce] Common issues I see: [weak summary / buried accomplishments / job duty lists instead of impact statements / no keyword optimization / LinkedIn that does not match resume] Create: (1) Resume audit checklist (20 checkpoints — structure → content → impact statements → ATS keywords → formatting → dates and gaps), (2) LinkedIn profile audit (15 checkpoints — headline / summary / experience / skills / recommendations / activity), (3) How to give feedback that is honest but does not discourage the client, (4) Before/after rewrite examples for the 3 most common resume mistakes, (5) How to prioritize feedback — what to fix first vs. what is nice to have. Clients need honest feedback delivered with care — this is where coaching skill matters most.
Pro tip: The most powerful resume rewrite is turning "managed a team" into "led a team of 8 engineers that shipped X and reduced Y by Z%." Every bullet should have a number or a named outcome.

Prompt 6 — Interview Preparation Session Framework

Design an interview preparation coaching session framework. Interview type the client is preparing for: [behavioral / technical / case / panel / executive / culture-fit / salary negotiation] Role level: [entry / mid / senior / executive] Timeline to interview: [48 hours / 1 week / 2+ weeks] Client's biggest weakness in interviews: [nerves / rambling answers / underselling themselves / not researching the company / weak salary negotiation] Create: (1) Pre-session: what the client should prepare and send before we meet, (2) Session structure — how to spend the time most effectively, (3) STAR framework coaching questions — how to help clients build stories without coaching them on what to say, (4) The 10 most common behavioral questions for this role level, (5) Mock interview protocol (how to run a realistic simulation without it feeling fake), (6) Post-session practice homework. Good interview coaching is about drawing out what clients already know — not scripting answers for them.

3. Content & Thought Leadership

Prompt 7 — Blog or Newsletter Content

Write a blog post or newsletter article on a career coaching topic. Topics: [the resume mistake that is keeping you from getting interviews / why your job search is not working (and how to fix it) / how to negotiate salary without the awkward conversation / the career pivot most people get wrong / what hiring managers actually look for in interviews / when to quit your job] Chosen topic: [paste] My angle: [what do I believe about this that is more nuanced than what is commonly said] Who is reading this: [job seekers / career changers / ambitious professionals / clients who follow me] Write: Hook (first 2–3 sentences that name their exact situation) → the conventional wisdom / what everyone says → what I actually think / what the data or experience shows → the practical implication → one action to take this week. Under 700 words. Conversational, specific, no career coaching clichés.

Prompt 8 — Free Resource Creation

Help me create a free resource that attracts my ideal coaching clients. My ideal client: [describe — what are they struggling with, what do they search for] Resource format: [checklist / guide / template / email course / worksheet / quiz] Topic: [the exact job search system I use with clients / how to find a job through networking / the salary negotiation script / the career change roadmap / the executive LinkedIn formula] Create: (1) Title options (5 variations — specific, outcome-focused, not generic), (2) Outline (section by section — what it covers and in what order), (3) The first section in full (so I can model the rest), (4) Where and how to offer it (landing page, LinkedIn, email signature), (5) One lead-nurture email to send immediately after someone downloads it. The best lead magnets solve a specific problem so well that clients think "if this is free, the coaching must be incredible."

4. Practice Management

Prompt 9 — Coaching Package Design

Help me design and write copy for my career coaching packages. Current services: [1:1 sessions / packages / group programs / courses / resume reviews — describe what you offer] Pricing: [current pricing or where you want to go] Ideal client transformation: [from where to where in how long] What makes my approach different: [methodology / experience / specific results / specialty niche] Deliver: (1) Package options (3 tiers — how to structure them so the middle option is the most compelling), (2) Package names (specific, outcome-focused — not "Gold," "Platinum"), (3) Sales page copy for your highest-value package (lead with the transformation, not the sessions), (4) FAQ section (6 questions that address what stops people from buying), (5) How to raise your rates without losing clients — the conversation script.
Pro tip: Package names that describe the outcome ("Land Your Dream Job in 90 Days") convert better than names that describe the product ("8-Session Coaching Package").

Prompt 10 — Client Testimonial and Case Study System

Build a system for collecting and using client testimonials and case studies. When I currently ask for testimonials: [at end of engagement / when they share good news / rarely / never] What stops me from asking: [feels awkward / forgot / not sure how] What I want testimonials for: [website / LinkedIn / proposals / social media] Create: (1) The exact message to send at the end of an engagement to request a testimonial (warm, specific question that gets a detailed response — not "please leave a review"), (2) The follow-up if they said yes but have not sent it yet, (3) 3 testimonial questions that surface before/after stories (not just "great coach!"), (4) How to structure a case study from a client success story (no client names needed — the situation and outcome are enough), (5) Where and how to use testimonials most effectively in your marketing. Testimonials you collect systematically are 10x better than the ones you get randomly.

5. Salary Negotiation Coaching

Prompt 11 — Salary Negotiation Session Prep

Prepare me for coaching a client through a salary negotiation. Client situation: [offer received / currently employed and asking for raise / counter-offer in hand / return from leave negotiating new terms] Their level: [entry / mid / senior / executive] Their hesitation: [afraid to negotiate / does not know what to ask for / worried about offer being rescinded / cultural or gender-related barriers] What I know about the offer: [salary / total comp / competing offer if any] Create: (1) Session framework for a salary negotiation coaching session, (2) The questions to ask to understand what success looks like for this client, (3) Key negotiation principles to coach on (anchoring / silence / the counter-offer script / what to negotiate beyond salary), (4) Role-play script for the actual negotiation conversation, (5) What to say if they are afraid of losing the offer (statistics on offer rescissions — rare and almost always avoidable), (6) How to debrief after the negotiation regardless of outcome.

6. Niche Development

Prompt 12 — Niche Positioning for Career Coaches

Help me define and communicate my career coaching niche. My background: [years in coaching / what industries or roles I have worked in / credentials] Clients I do my best work with: [be specific — not "anyone in transition"] Results I have seen with these clients: [describe outcomes] What I love about coaching: [what aspect of this work energizes you] What I avoid or struggle with: [be honest] Define: (1) My specific niche (not "career coaching" — the specific audience + specific transformation + specific context), (2) Why this niche is underserved or why I am distinctively positioned for it, (3) My positioning statement for LinkedIn and my website, (4) Content pillars for my niche (3 themes I should own), (5) The referral partner who most naturally sends me ideal clients, (6) How narrowing my niche will grow my practice rather than limit it.

7. Professional Development

Prompt 13 — Coaching Skill Practice

Help me practice and develop a specific coaching skill. Skill I want to develop: [powerful questioning / active listening / reframing / goal-setting / accountability / working with limiting beliefs / managing a difficult client / giving hard feedback / staying neutral] Create: (1) What this skill actually means in practice (not just the concept), (2) The most common way coaches fail at this skill, (3) 5 practice scenarios with coaching prompts (how would I handle this moment?), (4) How to self-assess after a session whether I used this skill effectively, (5) The one habit that most improves this skill over time. Coaching mastery comes from deliberate practice of specific skills — not just more coaching hours.

Prompt 14 — Supervision and Peer Consultation Prep

Help me prepare for supervision or peer consultation as a career coach. Case to discuss (no client names): [general situation — e.g., "client who keeps sabotaging every job opportunity and is not aware of it" / "client who says they want change but resists every action step" / "client who became dependent on coaching for decisions"] What I have tried: [approaches, interventions] Where I am stuck or uncertain: [what is not working / what I am not sure about] What I want from supervision: [a new perspective / a specific technique / validation / ethical guidance] Structure my case presentation: (1) Brief situation summary (2 sentences, no identifying details), (2) My current hypothesis about what is happening, (3) What I have tried and the response, (4) Specific questions I want to explore. Good supervision happens when the coach arrives prepared — vague cases produce vague guidance.

Prompt 15 — Annual Practice Review

Help me review my career coaching practice this year. Clients coached: [number] | Average engagement length: [sessions] | Client completion rate: [%] Revenue: [$] | Best referral source: [describe] Client success story that most energized me: [describe the situation] Client situation that was hardest: [describe — no names] What I want more of: [type of work / type of client] What I want less of: [be honest] Review: (1) My best-fit client profile — what patterns describe the clients I do my best work with, (2) Where my marketing is working vs. where it is generating the wrong clients, (3) The revenue gap between where I am and my goal — what single change would have the biggest impact, (4) My coaching skill development priority for next year, (5) The honest answer: what belief about myself or my practice is most limiting my growth right now?
