15 ChatGPT Prompts for Content Creators

Scroll-stopping hooks, repurposing systems, and content calendars — built for creators who want to publish more without burning out.

The best creators aren't working harder than everyone else. They have better systems. These 15 ChatGPT prompts are designed to eliminate the most time-consuming parts of content creation — coming up with ideas, writing hooks, repurposing content, and planning what to post next.

These work for any platform: YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Substack, or blogs. Copy them into ChatGPT (free or Plus), Claude, or Gemini.

Hooks That Stop the Scroll

1. The Hook Generator

The first 3 seconds (or 3 words) determine whether anyone watches, reads, or clicks. This prompt creates hooks that work.

You are a viral content strategist who has studied the top 1% of performing posts on [platform]. Write 10 different opening hooks for a post about [your topic]. Each hook should use a different psychological trigger: curiosity gap, shocking stat, contrarian take, relatable pain, bold claim, story opener, question, before/after, list format, and direct challenge. Rate each one on a scale of 1-10 for scroll-stopping power and explain why.

2. The Pattern Interrupt

When your niche sounds the same, pattern interrupts make you memorable.

My content niche is [your niche]. The typical content in this niche always starts with [describe the common format/tone]. Write 5 hook variations that completely break this pattern — say something unexpected, counterintuitive, or that would make someone say "wait, really?" Force them to stop and read more.

3. The Platform-Specific Hook Converter

I have this core idea: "[your content idea]". Rewrite it as a platform-optimized hook for each of these: 1) Instagram caption (attention in first line before "more"), 2) TikTok video hook (first 2 seconds of speech), 3) YouTube title + thumbnail text, 4) LinkedIn post opener, 5) Twitter/X first tweet in a thread. Each should feel native to that platform.

Content Repurposing System

4. The One-to-Many Repurposer

The secret to posting consistently without burning out: create once, repurpose everywhere.

I just created [content type: YouTube video / blog post / podcast episode / newsletter] about [topic]. Here's the key content: [paste transcript, outline, or summary]. Repurpose this into: 1) A 5-tweet thread, 2) An Instagram carousel (10 slides with title + 1 key point each), 3) A LinkedIn article intro (300 words), 4) 3 short-form video scripts (60 seconds each, different angles), 5) An email newsletter version. Give me all 5 formats.

5. The Quote Extractor

Shareable quotes from your existing content that you can post as standalone graphics.

Read through this content: [paste your content]. Extract 10 sentences or phrases that work as standalone shareable quotes — they should be insightful, counterintuitive, or emotionally resonant without needing context. Format them as quote graphics: add the quote, then a 1-sentence attribution line that sounds authoritative. Make them feel like something people would screenshot.

6. The Series Generator

I want to create a content series around [topic] for my [platform] audience. The series should have 8-10 episodes/posts. Design the entire series arc: 1) Give each episode a specific title, 2) Explain the angle for each (what unique value does THIS episode deliver), 3) Suggest the best posting order for maximum audience retention, 4) Identify which episode should be the "free sample" to promote the full series.

Content Strategy & Planning

7. The 30-Day Content Calendar

Create a 30-day content calendar for my [platform] account about [niche]. My content pillars are: [list 3-4 topics you post about]. The mix should be: 40% educational/value, 30% entertaining/relatable, 20% behind-the-scenes/personal, 10% promotional. Include: the post type (video/carousel/text/story), the hook, and 2-3 bullet points of what the content covers. Format as a table.

8. The Audience Research Prompt

Most creators guess what their audience wants. This prompt helps you know.

I create content about [topic] for [audience description]. Help me deeply understand what this audience actually wants by: 1) Listing their top 5 frustrations related to [topic], 2) Identifying the questions they search for most, 3) Describing their dream outcome from consuming content like mine, 4) Naming 3 misconceptions they have that my content should address, 5) Suggesting the content format they're most likely to engage with based on their behavior patterns. Be specific — no generic answers.

9. The Trend Newsjacker

There's a trending topic right now: [describe the trend]. My niche is [your niche]. Help me newsjack this trend by: 1) Identifying the connection between this trend and my audience's interests, 2) Writing a post hook that references the trend but ties it to my niche, 3) Suggesting an angle that's not obvious — something that would make people think "I didn't think about it that way." The goal is to be relevant without being desperate or off-brand.

Writing & Editing

10. The Engagement Optimizer

Most posts die because they don't ask for anything. This prompt fixes that.

Here's my draft post: [paste post]. Improve it by: 1) Strengthening the hook (first line must make people stop), 2) Adding a specific curiosity gap if missing, 3) Making the value clearer in the middle section, 4) Adding a genuine engagement question at the end (not "what do you think?" — something specific that generates diverse answers), 5) Suggesting 1 thing to cut that slows it down. Show me the before and after.

11. The Caption Formula Applicator

I need a caption for [describe your content: image/video/reel about X]. Write versions using these proven caption formulas: 1) Problem-Agitate-Solve, 2) Before-After-Bridge, 3) Story + Lesson, 4) Listicle with a twist, 5) Contrarian take + evidence. For each, write the full caption ready to post. I'll pick the one that fits best.

12. The CTA Writer

Generic CTAs ("link in bio") convert poorly. Specific CTAs that explain the benefit convert 3-5x better.

I'm promoting [product/service/newsletter/channel] at the end of my content. Write 10 call-to-action variations that: 1) Explain specifically what they get, 2) Create mild urgency without being pushy, 3) Sound like a natural recommendation from a friend, not an ad. Avoid: "click the link," "check it out," and "if you're interested." Write for [platform].

Monetization & Growth

13. The Collaboration Pitch Writer

I want to pitch a collaboration/sponsorship to [type of brand]. My platform: [platform]. My stats: [followers, avg views, engagement rate]. My niche: [topic]. Write a cold pitch email that: 1) Opens with a specific compliment about their brand, 2) Shows I understand their target customer, 3) Proposes a specific collaboration format (not vague "let's work together"), 4) Includes a brief media kit mention, 5) Has a clear next step that's low-commitment. Keep it under 200 words.

14. The Viral Idea Generator

Generate 10 content ideas for my [niche] account that have viral potential. For each idea: 1) Give me the hook/title, 2) Explain WHY it has viral potential (what emotional trigger does it hit?), 3) Identify the format that works best (video, carousel, thread, etc.), 4) Rate the viral potential 1-10. Focus on ideas that are counterintuitive, emotionally charged, or would make people tag a friend.

15. The Email List Builder

I want to start building an email list from my [platform] audience. I create content about [topic]. Help me: 1) Design a lead magnet concept (a free resource so valuable they'd pay for it), 2) Write the CTA I'll use in my content to promote it (varies by platform), 3) Write the welcome email new subscribers get, 4) Outline a 5-email nurture sequence that builds trust before I promote anything paid. Be specific — "a PDF" is not enough; I need the exact title and contents of the lead magnet.
Pro tip: The best creators have a "content engine" — a weekly system for ideation, creation, and repurposing. Use these prompts to design your system once, then run it on autopilot.
