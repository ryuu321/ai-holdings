ChatGPT Prompts for B2B Marketers

15 free prompts to create better account-based campaigns, LinkedIn content, sales enablement, and demand generation — faster.

B2B marketing is won or lost on specificity. The marketer who understands the buyer's exact pain point and can communicate it in the buyer's language outperforms everyone running generic content. These 15 prompts help you write sharper campaigns, enable your sales team, and create content that actually converts.

1. Account-Based Marketing

Prompt 1 — ICP and Buyer Persona

Help me define my ideal customer profile (ICP) and primary buyer persona. Product/service: [what you sell — be specific] Current best customers: [describe in terms of company size, industry, growth stage, and what they have in common] The problem your product solves: [in the customer's language — not your marketing language] Who in the organization makes the decision: [title, role, what they care about professionally] Who influences the decision: [champions, blockers, budget holders] What a bad-fit customer looks like: [who wastes your team's time] Produce: (1) ICP definition (firmographics + behavioral signals that indicate fit), (2) primary buyer persona profile with goals, fears, and success metrics, (3) the 3 objections this persona raises most often, and (4) the trigger events that make this persona go from "interested" to "actively buying."
Pro tip: Your ICP is not who you want to sell to — it's who you have actually won and retained at the highest rate. Pull your best 20 customers and find the pattern before building a persona from scratch.

Prompt 2 — ABM Campaign Plan for a Target Account

Help me build an account-based marketing campaign for a specific target account. Target account type: [industry / company size / tech stack if relevant — no real company names needed] Buying committee roles at this account: [list 3–5 titles involved in the decision] Known pain points for this account: [what problem is urgent for them right now] Where they are in the buying journey: [unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / comparing vendors / close to decision] Channels available: [LinkedIn / email / paid / events / direct mail / sales outreach] Timeline: [campaign duration] Write a multi-touch ABM campaign plan: (1) content for each buying committee member, (2) channel sequence and timing, (3) personalization strategy for outreach, (4) how to measure account engagement, and (5) handoff triggers from marketing to sales.

Prompt 3 — Personalized Outreach Sequence

Help me write a personalized outreach sequence for a target account. Persona I'm reaching: [title and role] What their world looks like right now: [company initiatives, industry challenges, recent news — speak to their reality] What I want them to do: [book a call / download a resource / attend a webinar / respond] Value prop most relevant to this persona: [why this matters to them specifically] Sequence length: [number of touches over how many days] Channels: [email / LinkedIn DM / cold call / combination] Write the full sequence: subject lines, opening hooks, body copy, CTA for each touch. Each message should read as personal, not templated. No generic "just following up" messages.
Pro tip: The highest-performing B2B outreach sequences lead with a specific observation about the prospect's business — not about your product. "I noticed you just announced a new product launch" opens doors. "We help companies like yours" doesn't.

2. Content Marketing

Prompt 4 — LinkedIn Content Strategy

Help me build a LinkedIn content strategy for B2B brand awareness and demand generation. Company / personal brand: [company type or individual's role] Target audience on LinkedIn: [title, industry, company size] Business goal for LinkedIn: [brand awareness / pipeline generation / hiring / thought leadership] Topics my audience cares about: [list 4–5 — business problems, industry trends, decisions they face] Posting frequency goal: [times per week] Voice: [educational / provocative / data-driven / storytelling / practical how-to] Write: (1) content pillar framework (3–4 themes with rationale), (2) a 4-week content calendar with post ideas for each day, (3) 3 post formats that perform well for B2B audiences, (4) how to write a hook for LinkedIn that gets past the "more" cutoff, and (5) engagement strategy — how to use comments and reactions to grow reach.

Prompt 5 — Long-Form Content Brief

Help me write a content brief for a long-form B2B asset. Asset type: [white paper / ebook / ultimate guide / original research report / case study] Target persona: [title, role, what they're trying to solve] Search intent or content angle: [are they searching for this / will it be gated / LinkedIn amplification] Core argument or insight: [what does this piece teach that they can't get elsewhere] Data or examples I can include: [proprietary data, case studies, expert quotes — placeholder OK] Length goal: [approximate word count or pages] Write: (1) working title and 3 title variants, (2) executive summary hook, (3) full outline with section headers and a 2-line description of each section, (4) the key insight or data point in each section, and (5) CTA strategy — what should happen after they read this.

Prompt 6 — Customer Story / Case Study

Help me write a B2B customer case study. Customer type: [industry / company size — no real names needed unless you're using them publicly] Problem before your product: [what their world looked like — specific pain, not generic] Why they chose your product: [what made them pick you over alternatives] Implementation: [how long, what it took, any friction] Results achieved: [quantified if possible — time saved, revenue generated, cost reduced, risk reduced] Quote you have or can get: [what the customer actually said] Write a case study in the "challenge / solution / results" format. Include a one-sentence summary (for previews), a pull quote, and a section that speaks directly to other prospects in the same situation.
Pro tip: The most persuasive case studies are written for a specific persona reading them — not for everyone. A case study titled "How a 50-person SaaS company reduced churn by 30%" will convert better for that segment than a generic success story.

3. Sales Enablement

Prompt 7 — Competitive Battle Card

Help me write a sales battle card against a key competitor. Your product: [what you sell and your primary differentiator] Competitor: [describe their product and what they're known for — no need for real names] Where you win: [specific scenarios where you beat them] Where you lose: [honest — where they are legitimately stronger] Common objections sales hears in competitive deals: [list] What prospects who chose the competitor say afterward: [if you have this data] Write a one-page battle card with: (1) when to position against this competitor vs. when to ignore them, (2) 3 questions to ask that expose their weaknesses without attacking them directly, (3) how to respond to "we're already using [competitor]," and (4) the proof points that matter most in head-to-head deals.

Prompt 8 — Sales Deck Narrative

Help me rewrite our sales deck narrative. Current problem with our deck: [too product-focused / no clear story arc / losing audience by slide 3 / not tailored to persona] Our product: [what it does] The buyer's problem we solve: [in their language] How the conversation typically goes wrong: [where deals stall] What the ideal deck would achieve by the end: [specific outcome — demo booked / agreement on next step / RFP submission] Write a narrative outline for a 10–15 slide sales deck: (1) opening slide that earns attention, (2) problem framing that makes the buyer feel understood, (3) the "aha" insight that reframes the problem, (4) solution positioned as the logical answer to the reframed problem, (5) proof section, (6) next step CTA. Include the actual words for each slide's headline.

Prompt 9 — Email Templates for Sales Team

Help me create email templates for our sales team to use in specific scenarios. Scenarios to cover: [list 3–5 — e.g., post-demo follow-up / proposal sent follow-up / re-engagement of cold deal / introduction to a new stakeholder / handling a "not right now" response] Our product and value prop: [brief] Tone that fits our brand: [consultative / direct / warm / enterprise-formal] What typically causes deals to stall at each stage: [be honest] For each scenario, write: (1) subject line (3 variants), (2) email body (under 150 words — B2B buyers don't read essays), (3) specific CTA, and (4) one personalization placeholder to make each email feel unique.
Pro tip: The best sales emails are short enough that the prospect can decide to respond before they reach the bottom. If your email needs scrolling, it needs editing.

4. Demand Generation

Prompt 10 — Webinar and Event Strategy

Help me plan a B2B demand generation webinar. Topic: [the specific problem or trend you want to address] Target audience: [title, industry, company size] Format: [presentation / panel / live demo / workshop / AMA] Guest or speakers: [internal only / external thought leaders / customer panel] Pipeline goal: [what should happen 30 days after the webinar — MQLs, demo requests, account engagement] Write: (1) webinar title and 3 title variants optimized for registrations, (2) agenda outline with talking points for each segment, (3) pre-event email sequence (3 emails), (4) how to generate value for non-attendees (recording, recap, clips), and (5) post-event follow-up sequence for attendees vs. no-shows.

Prompt 11 — Paid Campaign Copy

Help me write copy for a B2B paid campaign. Channel: [LinkedIn / Google / Meta / programmatic display] Goal: [lead gen / brand awareness / retargeting / event registration] Target audience: [title / company size / industry / behavior — be specific for LinkedIn] Offer: [what they get — ebook / demo / free trial / assessment / event registration] Key message: [what should make them click] Write: (1) 3 headline variants (under 70 characters each), (2) 2 body copy variants (under 150 characters each for social / under 300 words for Google RSA), (3) CTA copy (5 options), and (4) a landing page headline and above-the-fold copy that matches the ad promise.

Prompt 12 — Marketing-to-Sales Handoff Process

Help me improve our marketing-to-sales handoff process. Current problem: [leads not being followed up / sales ignores MQLs / poor conversion from MQL to SQL / no feedback loop from sales to marketing] Current handoff process: [how it works now] What sales says about the leads marketing sends: [the actual feedback] What marketing believes about how sales uses leads: [the actual perception] Our lead volume: [approximate MQLs per month] Write: (1) a revised lead scoring model that sales would trust, (2) the information sales needs to follow up effectively (and how marketing should provide it), (3) an SLA framework that makes both teams accountable, and (4) the one thing marketing should change immediately based on what sales has been saying.
Pro tip: Most MQL-to-SQL conversion problems are not a lead quality problem — they're a speed-to-contact problem. If sales isn't responding to MQLs within 5 minutes during business hours, fix that before rebuilding your scoring model.

5. Measurement and Reporting

Prompt 13 — Marketing Attribution Narrative

Help me communicate marketing's contribution to revenue to leadership. Leadership's current concern about marketing: [too much spend / unclear ROI / attribution disagreement with sales / request to cut budget] Metrics I have: [pipeline generated / influenced revenue / MQL volume / content engagement / brand awareness — list what you can actually report on] The story the numbers tell: [what is working, what is not] What I want leadership to understand: [the core argument] What I'm asking for: [budget / headcount / patience / a new measurement approach] Write: (1) a one-page marketing performance narrative for an executive audience, (2) how to frame "influenced pipeline" vs. "sourced pipeline" in a way non-marketers understand, (3) the honest conversation about what marketing can and cannot control, and (4) the leading indicators I should be reporting to build credibility before deals close.

Prompt 14 — Quarterly Marketing Plan

Help me write a quarterly marketing plan. Company stage: [early-stage / growth / enterprise] Quarter goals: [pipeline goal / revenue goal / product launch / market expansion / retention focus] Budget available: [rough range or "limited"] Team size: [number of people in marketing] What worked last quarter: [channels, campaigns, content types] What did not work last quarter: [be specific] Write a quarter plan with: (1) the 3 priorities and why (not 10 priorities), (2) campaign calendar with key milestones, (3) resource allocation by channel, (4) how we'll measure success at mid-quarter, and (5) what we'll cut if we're behind on pipeline at the 6-week mark.

Prompt 15 — Post-Campaign Analysis

Help me write a post-campaign analysis and recommendations. Campaign: [type / goal / channels / duration] Results vs. goals: [what happened — be specific and honest about underperformance] What worked: [channels, messages, creative, timing] What did not: [same categories — be honest] Why it underperformed (if it did): [hypothesis — not just "the market was tough"] What I'd do differently: [specific changes] Write a post-campaign document: (1) executive summary (5 sentences), (2) detailed channel-by-channel breakdown, (3) lessons with confidence level (high / medium / low certainty), (4) specific recommendations for the next campaign, and (5) what we should test to validate our hypothesis about what didn't work.
