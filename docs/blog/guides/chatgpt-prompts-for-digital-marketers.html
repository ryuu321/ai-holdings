ChatGPT Prompts for Digital Marketers

15 free prompts to write better ad copy, plan campaigns, analyze competitors, and scale results — without the guesswork.

Digital marketing moves fast. AI won't replace your strategic thinking, but it can eliminate the hours you spend staring at blank docs, writing 10 variations of ad copy, or trying to structure a campaign from scratch. These 15 prompts are built for working marketers — specific, practical, and ready to use today.

1. Ad Copy & Campaign Messaging

Prompt 1 — Multi-Variant Ad Copy Generator

You are a direct-response copywriter. I'm running a [Facebook/Google/LinkedIn] ad campaign for [product/service]. My target audience is [describe: age, role, pain point]. The campaign objective is [awareness/leads/conversions]. Write 5 ad copy variations. For each, give: (1) headline (under 40 chars), (2) primary text (under 125 chars), (3) description (under 30 chars), (4) the psychological trigger used. Label them A–E. Make each distinct in angle (problem-focused, outcome-focused, social proof, curiosity, fear of missing out).
Pro tip: Run all 5 in a split test. Let the data pick the winner — don't guess based on your gut.

Prompt 2 — Landing Page Copy Framework

Write a high-converting landing page for [product/service]. Target: [audience]. Main problem solved: [describe]. Unique mechanism: [what makes this different]. Price: [price point]. Outcome: [what they get]. Structure: Hero headline + subhead → 3 pain points → solution intro → 3 key benefits (with proof points) → social proof section (suggest 3 testimonial types) → FAQ (5 objections/answers) → CTA. Write in a conversational, direct tone. No fluff.

2. SEO & Content Marketing

Prompt 3 — Content Cluster Strategy Builder

I want to rank for [primary keyword] in [niche]. Build a complete content cluster strategy: 1. Pillar page topic and angle 2. 10 supporting cluster articles (with keyword, search intent, recommended word count) 3. Internal linking structure (which clusters link to pillar, which clusters cross-link) 4. Quick-win articles (lower competition, faster to rank) 5. Content repurposing plan (which pieces become emails, social posts, videos) My domain is [new/established]. Current DA: [estimate if known].
Pro tip: Build the pillar page first, then create clusters in order of search volume. Internal links compound over time.

Prompt 4 — Blog Post Outline with SEO Architecture

Write an SEO-optimized outline for an article targeting "[target keyword]". Search intent: [informational/transactional/commercial]. Include: Title tag (under 60 chars) → Meta description (under 155 chars) → H1 → Introduction hook → 5–7 H2 sections with H3 subsections → Featured snippet opportunity (question + 40-word answer box) → FAQ schema section (5 Q&As) → Conclusion + CTA. Suggest related keywords to sprinkle naturally.

3. Competitor & Market Analysis

Prompt 5 — Competitor Messaging Analysis

I'm analyzing competitors in the [niche] market. Here are the homepage headlines and key messaging from my top 3 competitors: [Competitor 1]: [their headline/positioning] [Competitor 2]: [their headline/positioning] [Competitor 3]: [their headline/positioning] Analyze: (1) What messaging angles are all 3 using? (2) What are they NOT saying — the positioning gap? (3) What customer fears/desires are they targeting? (4) Where is the opportunity for me to differentiate? Suggest 3 unique positioning angles I could own.
Pro tip: Copy competitor homepage H1s and ad headlines directly into this prompt. Real data beats assumptions.

Prompt 6 — Customer Research Synthesizer

I've collected the following customer research for [product/service]: Reviews: [paste 5–10 customer reviews] Survey responses: [paste key responses] Sales call notes: [describe common objections/outcomes] Synthesize: (1) Top 3 jobs-to-be-done, (2) Most common emotional language (exact phrases to use in copy), (3) Primary objections with suggested rebuttals, (4) The #1 outcome customers actually want (not what they say they want), (5) 3 headline angles derived directly from their words.

4. Email Marketing

Prompt 7 — Email Welcome Sequence

Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers who opted in for [lead magnet]. They are [audience description] interested in [topic]. End goal: convert them to [product/offer] by email 5. For each email: Subject line (+ 1 alternative) → Preview text → Body (300–400 words) → CTA. Tone: [conversational/professional/energetic]. Email 1: value delivery. Email 2: story + credibility. Email 3: problem deepening. Email 4: solution reveal. Email 5: offer + urgency.
Pro tip: Email 2 (story + credibility) typically gets the highest open rate when the subject line references the story hook from Email 1.

Prompt 8 — Re-Engagement Campaign

Write a 3-email re-engagement campaign for subscribers who haven't opened in [60/90/120] days. Product/service: [describe]. Audience: [describe]. Email 1: Curiosity subject line + "are you still there?" hook + value reminder + easy CTA. Email 2: "Last chance" framing + concrete benefit statement + simple poll/question. Email 3: Soft breakup email with a final offer or content gift. Include subject line variants for each. Flag anyone who doesn't engage for list cleaning.

5. Paid Media & Performance

Prompt 9 — Google Ads Keyword Strategy

I'm building a Google Ads campaign for [product/service]. Budget: [$/month]. Target location: [geo]. Goal: [leads/sales/calls]. Generate: (1) 20 high-intent keywords organized by match type (exact/phrase/broad), (2) 10 negative keywords to exclude, (3) 3 ad group themes with 5 keywords each, (4) 2 RSA headlines and descriptions for each ad group, (5) Recommended bidding strategy with rationale given my budget and goal.
Pro tip: Start exact match only for the first 2 weeks. Expand to phrase after you have conversion data. Never start broad without data.

Prompt 10 — Meta Ads Audience Strategy

I'm running Meta ads for [product/service]. Price point: [price]. Ideal customer: [describe demographics, interests, behaviors, pain points]. Build my audience strategy: (1) 3 cold audiences (interest-based, with specific targeting suggestions), (2) Lookalike audience structure (what seed audience to use and why), (3) Retargeting stack (website visitors 30-day, video viewers 50%, past purchasers for upsell), (4) Budget allocation recommendation across cold/warm/hot, (5) Which creative format to test first for each audience tier and why.

6. Analytics & Reporting

Prompt 11 — Campaign Performance Analysis

Here is my campaign performance data for [time period]: [Paste metrics: impressions, clicks, CTR, CPC, conversions, CVR, CPA, ROAS, revenue] Analyze and tell me: (1) What's working — top performers and why, (2) What's underperforming — root cause analysis, (3) Budget reallocation recommendation, (4) The single highest-leverage test to run next week, (5) Red flags I should be worried about. Be direct. Don't sugarcoat the data.
Pro tip: Paste raw CSV data if your AI tool accepts it. Pattern recognition across 50+ rows beats summarizing manually.

Prompt 12 — Executive Marketing Report

Write a concise executive marketing report for [month]. Audience: CEO/leadership team. Data: [paste key metrics] Structure: 3-bullet performance summary → Top win this month → Biggest challenge + what we're doing about it → Next month priority actions (3 bullets) → KPI dashboard summary table. Tone: Confident, concise. No jargon. Translate metrics into business impact. Keep it to one page.

7. Strategy & Growth

Prompt 13 — Full-Funnel Marketing Strategy

Build a full-funnel digital marketing strategy for [business type]. Product/service: [describe]. Budget: [$/month]. Team size: [solo/small team/agency]. Current channels: [list]. Map the funnel: (1) Awareness — top channels, content types, KPIs, (2) Consideration — nurture tactics, retargeting, email strategy, (3) Conversion — landing page priorities, offer structure, checkout optimization, (4) Retention — post-purchase sequence, upsell triggers, loyalty tactics. Prioritize highest-ROI activities given my budget and team size.

Prompt 14 — Channel Prioritization Framework

I have [$/month] to invest in digital marketing for [business]. My goal: [X leads/X sales] per month. I'm considering: [list channels: SEO, Google Ads, Meta Ads, email, content, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc.]. Evaluate each channel on: (1) Time to first result, (2) Cost to acquire a customer, (3) Scalability, (4) Fit for my product/audience. Score each 1–5 and recommend where to allocate budget in month 1, month 3, and month 6. Explain the sequencing logic.
Pro tip: Almost every business should start with the channel where their best customers already spend time — then expand.

Prompt 15 — Marketing Experiment Design

I want to run a structured marketing experiment. Hypothesis: [state what you think is true and why]. Current baseline: [metric + current number]. Target: [goal metric]. Design the experiment: (1) Control vs. treatment — what exactly changes, (2) Sample size needed for statistical significance, (3) Duration and when to call it, (4) How to isolate variables (what NOT to change), (5) Decision framework — if X happens, do Y; if A happens, do B. Make it rigorous but practical for a [company size] team.
