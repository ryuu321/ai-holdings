ChatGPT Prompts for Chiropractors

15 free prompts to write better patient communications, grow your practice, and save hours on admin every week.

Chiropractors spend significant time on patient education, marketing, and administrative writing — much of which can be streamlined with AI. These 15 prompts help you communicate more clearly, attract more patients, and spend more time doing the hands-on work that matters.

Important: These prompts assist with communication and practice management — not with clinical diagnosis, treatment planning, or medical advice. Never input patient names, dates of birth, diagnoses, or any protected health information (PHI) into AI tools. All AI-generated health content must be reviewed for clinical accuracy before use. Consult your malpractice carrier and state board regarding appropriate marketing claims.

1. Patient Communication

Prompt 1 — New Patient Welcome Message

Write a new patient welcome email for my chiropractic practice. Practice name: [name] What I want new patients to feel: [welcomed / clear on what to expect / excited to start] My approach: [gentle / sports-focused / family / holistic / corrective care] First visit process: [what happens — forms, consultation, exam, first adjustment or not] Location / parking: [brief logistics] Write: Subject line → Warm welcome paragraph → What to expect at your first visit (step by step) → What to bring (insurance card, list of medications, comfortable clothing) → Parking/arrival instructions → "We look forward to meeting you" close. Under 300 words. Warm, professional, not clinical.
Pro tip: Most new chiropractic patients have some anxiety about their first adjustment. Name that feeling directly in your welcome email — it immediately builds trust.

Prompt 2 — Reactivation Email for Lapsed Patients

Write a reactivation email for patients who haven't visited in 6+ months. My practice: [name, specialty focus] Common reason patients drift away: [life gets busy / they felt better and stopped / cost concerns / forgot about maintenance care] What I want to offer: [no pressure — just checking in / a "welcome back" appointment / a specific reminder about ongoing care value] Tone: [warm and human — not salesy] Write a short email (under 200 words): Opening that acknowledges it has been a while (no guilt) → One sentence on why consistent care matters (general, non-clinical) → Easy next step (link to schedule or call us). Also write a 2-sentence SMS version.

Prompt 3 — Appointment Reminder + Care Instruction

Write appointment reminder messages for my chiropractic practice. Appointment type: [adjustment / new patient consultation / follow-up / wellness visit] Timing: [24 hours before / 1 hour before] Post-visit care reminders to include: [drink water / light movement / avoid heavy lifting / normal soreness is expected] Write: (1) Email reminder (friendly, under 150 words, includes appointment details placeholder + post-visit care tips), (2) SMS version (under 160 characters — just the essentials), (3) Post-visit text (send same day — short, caring, asks how they're feeling + reminds them to book next visit).

2. Practice Marketing

Prompt 4 — Google Business Profile Posts

Write 10 Google Business Profile posts for my chiropractic clinic. My practice specialty: [family / sports / auto injury / prenatal / corrective care / all-ages wellness] My location: [city] Goal: [local SEO visibility / patient education / seasonal promotions / build trust] 10 post ideas, each under 200 words: Mix of — educational health tip → patient success theme (general, no names) → myth-busting → seasonal condition spotlight (back-to-school posture / winter sports injuries / holiday stress) → what to expect at first visit. Each post: hook sentence → body → CTA (book online / call us / learn more). No unverifiable medical claims.
Pro tip: Google Business posts that answer "what can chiropractic help with?" outperform promotional posts 3:1 for local search engagement.

Prompt 5 — Social Media Content Calendar

Build a 4-week social media content calendar for my chiropractic practice. Platforms: [Instagram / Facebook / both] My specialty focus: [describe] My audience: [families / athletes / office workers / seniors / prenatal] Content goal: [education / trust-building / patient referrals / local visibility] 28 post ideas (one per day): For each — platform, format (photo/reel/carousel/text), topic, caption hook, and hashtag set. Content mix: spinal health tips → ergonomics for daily life → exercise and posture → myth-busting → behind-the-scenes practice life → community involvement → patient education (general). No before/after claims, no specific medical outcome promises.

Prompt 6 — Local Partnership Outreach

Write outreach emails to build referral partnerships with local businesses. Partners I want to connect with: [personal trainers / yoga studios / massage therapists / physical therapists / OB-GYNs / pediatricians / sports coaches / ergonomic consultants] My practice: [specialty, patient focus, what makes me different] What I offer their clients: [complementary care for their patients] What I offer them: [cross-referrals / joint events / educational content] Write: (1) Email to a personal trainer (150 words — specific to their clients, propose something concrete), (2) Email to a massage therapist (different angle — collaborative care model), (3) Follow-up after no response (2 weeks — short and warm). Goal: coffee meeting or patient-sharing arrangement, not a sales pitch.

3. Patient Education

Prompt 7 — Health Condition Explainer

Write patient-friendly explanations for common conditions I treat. Write explanations for: [lower back pain / neck pain / sciatica / herniated disc / tech neck / headaches / shoulder tension / poor posture / hip misalignment — choose 3] For each: (1) What is it (plain language, no jargon) → (2) Common causes (daily habits, not anatomy lectures) → (3) How chiropractic care can help (general, not diagnostic) → (4) What patients can do at home between visits → (5) When to seek additional care. Each explanation: Under 250 words. Written for someone who has never seen a chiropractor. Accurate but accessible. No specific medical claims about cure or guaranteed outcomes.
Pro tip: Condition explainers become evergreen content — use them on your website FAQ, in patient packets, and as social posts over time.

Prompt 8 — Home Care Instructions

Write post-adjustment home care instruction sheets for patients. Conditions: [acute lower back pain / neck pain after car accident / chronic headaches / sports injury recovery / prenatal discomfort — choose 2] For each: (1) What to expect in the next 24–48 hours (soreness is normal — normalize it), (2) Helpful activities (gentle movement, heat/ice guidance, hydration), (3) Activities to avoid temporarily, (4) When to call the office (specific symptoms), (5) Simple exercises or stretches to support their adjustment. Format: Plain language, bullet points, short sentences. Print-ready. No clinical language patients won't understand.

4. Content Creation

Prompt 9 — Blog Post Outline

Write a blog post outline on [topic] for my chiropractic practice website. Topic options: [5 signs your desk setup is hurting your spine / what to expect at your first chiropractic visit / chiropractic care during pregnancy: what you need to know / can chiropractic help with headaches? / how to sleep better with back pain] Chosen topic: [paste] Target reader: [local resident who has never tried chiropractic / current patient / athlete] Goal: [SEO traffic / patient education / trust-building] Outline: H1 title (SEO-optimized) → Meta description (155 chars) → Hook intro (why this matters to them, not about the clinic) → 5–7 H2 sections with bullet points of what to cover → Conclusion with CTA. Include: 3 natural internal link opportunities (to other pages on my site) and the primary keyword to use throughout.

Prompt 10 — Patient Newsletter

Write a monthly patient newsletter for my chiropractic practice. Practice name: [name] Month/season: [describe — summer, back-to-school, etc.] Featured topic: [seasonal health focus — e.g., summer sports injuries / holiday travel and posture / winter activity tips] Patient audience: [families / mixed ages / athletes] Newsletter sections: (1) "From Dr. [Name]" — short personal note (100 words, warm and human, not clinical), (2) Health tip of the month — actionable, condition-related to the season, (3) Exercise or stretch of the month — simple, safe, can be done at home, (4) Practice news (new hours / upcoming event / new team member), (5) Reminder to book next appointment. Friendly, not medical-journal formal.

5. Practice Management

Prompt 11 — Patient Intake Form Cover Letter

Write a cover letter that accompanies my patient intake forms. My practice: [name, location] Forms they need to complete: [health history / insurance / HIPAA consent / payment policy] What I want patients to know before filling out forms: [we take their time seriously / forms help us help them / everything is confidential] My practice vibe: [warm family clinic / professional sports clinic / holistic wellness] Write: Short, warm letter (under 200 words) that: Thanks them for choosing us → Explains why we ask the questions we do (it genuinely helps us care for them better) → Reassures them about privacy (HIPAA) → Tells them how long forms take → Encourages them to call if they have questions. Remove any medical jargon.

Prompt 12 — Online Review Response Templates

Write review response templates for my chiropractic practice. Write responses for: (1) 5-star enthusiastic review — grateful, specific, warm (not generic "thank you for your review"), (2) 5-star review that mentions a specific outcome (back pain relief, headache improvement) — acknowledge without making medical claims, (3) 3-star review mentioning wait times — acknowledge, apologize, offer to fix, (4) 1-star review from someone unhappy with the front desk — professional, empathetic, invite offline conversation, (5) Fake or unfair review — calm, professional, factual, do not engage emotionally. Each response: Under 150 words. HIPAA note: Never confirm or deny someone was a patient in a public response.
Pro tip: Your response to a negative review is read by future patients more carefully than the review itself. Stay professional — your response is your audition.

6. Referral Network

Prompt 13 — Referral Thank-You System

Build a patient referral thank-you system for my practice. My referral incentive (if any): [personal thank-you card / no financial incentive — just gratitude / small gift / donation in their name] How referrals typically come in: [word of mouth / patient mentions at appointment / online] Write: (1) Handwritten note template to a patient who referred a friend (warm, personal, specific — under 75 words), (2) Email thank-you for email referrals (under 100 words), (3) 30-day check-in email to the referring patient asking how they're doing (nurtures the relationship, not transactional), (4) Script for the front desk to verbally thank a patient for a referral at their next appointment. Referral programs build themselves when you make people feel genuinely appreciated.

7. Professional Growth

Prompt 14 — CE Course Learning Notes

Help me process and apply what I learned from a continuing education course. Course topic: [describe — technique, condition management, practice management, nutrition, etc.] Key concepts I learned: [paste your rough notes or list 5–8 main points] How this applies to my current patient population: [describe] What I want to change or implement: [list 3–5 specific changes] Create: (1) Summary of the key concepts in plain language (for patient education use), (2) Implementation plan — specific changes to make in the next 30 days, (3) How to explain this new approach to existing patients who are used to my old method, (4) 3 patient education talking points I can use immediately.

Prompt 15 — Annual Practice Review

Help me review my chiropractic practice this year. Patient visits this year: [number] vs last year: [number] New patients: [number] | Patient retention rate: [%] | Referral source breakdown: [list] Revenue: [$] | Top performing service: [describe] Biggest challenge this year: [describe] Goal for next year: [specific number or outcome] Analyze: (1) My patient retention — what is keeping people coming back, and what might be pushing them away, (2) My best referral source — how to double down on it, (3) The gap between where I am and my goal — what would have to change, (4) One thing I can stop doing that takes time but generates little value, (5) The honest answer: what am I avoiding that would most accelerate growth?
