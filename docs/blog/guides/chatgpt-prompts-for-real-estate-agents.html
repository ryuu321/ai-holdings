15 ChatGPT Prompts for Real Estate Agents

From compelling listing descriptions to closing scripts to market analysis — use AI to handle the writing and research while you focus on closing deals.

The best real estate agents aren't the ones who work the hardest — they're the ones with the best systems. These 15 ChatGPT prompts give you systems for the parts of your business that eat time without directly building relationships: writing, research, and follow-up.

These work for residential agents, luxury specialists, commercial brokers, and property managers. Use them in ChatGPT (free or Plus), Claude, or Gemini. Always review and personalize the output before sending.

Listing Descriptions

1. The MLS Listing Writer

The best listing copy doesn't describe a house — it sells a lifestyle. This prompt writes it for you.

Write a compelling MLS listing description for this property: Address: [address or general location]. Bedrooms/Baths: [X bed / X bath]. Square footage: [SF]. Key features: [list 5-7 standout features: renovated kitchen, pool, cul-de-sac, school district, lot size, etc.]. Price: $[price]. Target buyer: [describe the ideal buyer: family, investor, downsizer, etc.]. Write a 200-250 word description that: 1) Opens with a lifestyle hook (not "Welcome to this beautiful home"), 2) Highlights the 3 most compelling features in the first paragraph, 3) Addresses the emotional appeal — what does it feel like to live here?, 4) Ends with a clear call to action. Avoid clichés like "meticulously maintained" and "move-in ready."

2. The Luxury Listing Elevator

Luxury listings need copy that justifies premium pricing. Generic descriptions leave money on the table.

Rewrite this listing description to position it for the luxury market. Original description: [paste your draft]. Price point: $[X]M. Target buyer: [high-net-worth individual / executive / international buyer / vacation home buyer]. Elevate the language to: 1) Use aspirational, sensory language (not just adjectives — describe what you see, feel, smell), 2) Emphasize exclusivity and rarity (limited supply, singular location, one-of-a-kind features), 3) Reference lifestyle elements that resonate with ultra-high-net-worth buyers, 4) Include a subtle scarcity element without being pushy. Keep it between 200-300 words. Avoid "luxury" as a descriptor — show it instead.

3. The Social Media Listing Carousel

I'm posting a new listing on Instagram as a carousel. Property: [brief description]. Asking price: $[price]. Top 5 features: [list them]. Write: 1) Slide 1 — headline hook that stops the scroll (5-8 words), 2) Slides 2-5 — one feature per slide with a headline (5 words) and 2-sentence description, 3) Slide 6 — a lifestyle image caption ("Imagine waking up to..."), 4) Final slide — CTA with my contact info placeholder. Also write the caption for the main post (under 150 words, includes relevant hashtags for [city/neighborhood]).

Client Communication

4. The Buyer Consultation Prep Script

The agent who asks better questions wins the relationship. This prompt prepares your consult before you walk in the door.

I have a buyer consultation with [describe what you know: first-time buyers / move-up buyers / investors / relocating from out of state]. They're looking for: [property type, price range, location, timeline]. Write: 1) 8 questions that uncover what they really want beyond their stated criteria (the emotional motivations), 2) 5 questions that reveal their financial situation without being intrusive, 3) A brief script for explaining my value as their agent (why they should work exclusively with me), 4) Objection responses for: "We want to look around first" and "We don't want to sign a buyer agreement." Keep each response under 3 sentences.

5. The Follow-Up Email Sequence

I showed [buyer name] properties [X] days/weeks ago and they've gone quiet. Their criteria: [describe what they're looking for]. Last thing I showed them: [describe what they saw]. Write a 3-email follow-up sequence: Email 1 (immediate): Check-in that adds value — share something new about the market or a property update. Email 2 (1 week later): Provide a brief market update relevant to their search area and price range. Email 3 (2 weeks later): A gentle "are you still in the market?" email that leaves the door open without pressure. Each email should be under 100 words. No "just checking in" openers.

6. The Difficult Conversation Handler

I need to have a difficult conversation with my client: [choose one — their offer was rejected and they're frustrated / the appraisal came in low / the inspection revealed major issues / they want to price too high / they're getting cold feet]. Help me: 1) Frame this conversation to lead with empathy before solutions, 2) Script the key talking points in plain language, 3) Anticipate their likely reaction and prepare a response, 4) Propose a concrete next step that keeps the deal moving, 5) End the conversation in a way that strengthens the relationship regardless of outcome. Give me a word-for-word opening sentence.

Lead Generation & Marketing

7. The Just Sold / Just Listed Postcard Copy

Direct mail still works in real estate — when the copy makes people actually read it.

Write postcard copy for a [just sold / just listed] mailer to [neighborhood name] residents. Property details: [address, bed/bath, sold price or list price]. The goal of this mailer: [generate seller leads / build brand awareness / demonstrate market activity]. Write: 1) A headline that creates curiosity or urgency (not "Your neighbor just sold!"), 2) 3-4 body sentences that position me as the local market expert, 3) A specific CTA (not "call me" — something like "See what your home is worth in today's market"), 4) A P.S. line — postscripts get read. Keep total under 100 words.

8. The Listing Presentation Script

I'm presenting to a seller who is interviewing 3 agents. Their property: [describe the property — location, type, approximate value]. Their concern: [choose — they want the highest price / they need to sell fast / they had a bad experience with an agent before / they're skeptical about your commission]. Write a 10-minute listing presentation script that: 1) Opens by focusing on their goals, not my credentials, 2) Presents my marketing strategy with specifics (not "maximum exposure"), 3) Explains my pricing strategy and how I arrived at my recommended price, 4) Addresses the specific concern they have, 5) Closes with a clear ask for the listing. Include natural transition phrases between sections.

9. The Email Drip Campaign for Seller Leads

Write a 4-email drip campaign for homeowners who downloaded my "What's My Home Worth?" guide 30 days ago and haven't replied to outreach. These are potential sellers 6-18 months out. Each email should: 1) Add genuine value (market update, home prep tip, timing insight), 2) Not ask for a meeting until email 4, 3) Build trust by demonstrating market expertise, 4) Be under 120 words each. Email subjects should feel personal, not salesy. Include subject line suggestions for each email.

Market Research & Analysis

10. The Market Report Generator

Monthly market reports position you as the local expert. They're also tedious to write — until now.

Write a monthly market update for [neighborhood/city] homeowners and buyers. Here are the key stats for [month]: Median sales price: $[X] ([+/-]% from last month / last year). Average days on market: [X]. Months of inventory: [X]. Number of sales: [X]. List-to-sale ratio: [X%]. Write a 250-300 word update that: 1) Opens with the one headline insight from this data, 2) Explains what these numbers mean for sellers and buyers separately, 3) Offers a forward-looking perspective on what's likely to happen next month, 4) Ends with a soft CTA. Tone: confident, data-informed, not alarmist.

11. The Competitive Market Analysis (CMA) Narrative

I'm presenting a CMA for a property at [address]. The comparables I've selected are: [list 3-4 comparable sales with address, sale price, SF, bed/bath, days on market, and any notes]. The subject property differs from comps in these ways: [describe key differences — better kitchen, smaller lot, better school district, etc.]. Write a CMA narrative (150-200 words) that: 1) Explains my comp selection methodology, 2) Notes relevant adjustments I made, 3) Justifies my recommended list price of $[X], 4) Presents it as a range if appropriate: $[X] to $[Y]. Write it for a seller audience — confident, evidence-based, not jargon-heavy.

12. The Neighborhood Expert Bio

Write a neighborhood specialist bio for my website and marketing materials. I specialize in [neighborhood/city name]. Facts about me: [years working in this area, number of transactions, local knowledge, community involvement, personal connection to the area if any]. Write: 1) A 150-word bio that positions me as THE expert for this neighborhood (not just "knowledgeable about the area"), 2) 5 specific, verifiable neighborhood facts that demonstrate local expertise (schools, walkability, hidden gems, market trends), 3) A tagline I can use in my marketing (under 10 words). Avoid generic real estate bio language like "passionate about helping families find their dream home."

Business Development

13. The Referral Ask Script

Most agents know they should ask for referrals. Most don't have a script that doesn't feel awkward.

Write a word-for-word referral conversation script for asking happy past clients for referrals. Context: I closed this transaction [X] months ago. They [bought / sold] a [type of property] at [price range]. Write a script for: 1) An in-person or phone call version (under 90 seconds when spoken aloud), 2) A text message version (under 60 words), 3) An email version with a subject line. The script should feel like a genuine conversation, not a sales pitch. Include a specific, easy ask — not "let me know if anyone needs an agent."

14. The Google Review Request Email

Write an email to a recent client asking them to leave a Google review. Client: [name]. Transaction: We [helped them buy / sell] a [property type] in [area]. Close date: [X] ago. Unique experience: [one specific memorable moment or result you delivered]. Write an email that: 1) Thanks them genuinely without being sycophantic, 2) Mentions a specific detail that shows you remember their transaction (not generic), 3) Explains why reviews matter to your business in 1 sentence, 4) Makes the ask simple with a direct link placeholder, 5) Gives them a starting point: "You could mention [specific thing about the experience]." Under 120 words.

15. The Annual Client Touch Plan

Design a 12-month client retention touch plan for past clients (both buyers and sellers). Goal: stay top of mind so they call me when they're ready to transact again and refer me proactively. Budget: $0-$50/month for this client. Include: 1) Monthly touch point type (email, text, handwritten card, call, market update, gift), 2) The specific content or reason for each touch — not "market update" but "Q1 market report for their neighborhood with their home's estimated value", 3) 2-3 high-touch moments that stand out (anniversaries, life events), 4) Seasonal or event-based touches that feel natural, not salesy. Format as a month-by-month calendar.
Pro tip: Start with Prompt 1 (MLS Listing Writer) on your next listing. Most agents waste 2-3 hours writing descriptions. Use this prompt to get a strong first draft in 60 seconds, then spend 15 minutes personalizing it. Over a year with 20 listings, that's 30+ hours back.
