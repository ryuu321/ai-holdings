ChatGPT Prompts for Ecommerce Sellers

15 free prompts to write better product pages, run smarter ads, build email sequences that convert, and scale your Shopify or DTC brand with AI.

Ecommerce is a conversion optimization game. The brands winning in 2026 are using AI to write faster, test more angles, and build customer relationships at scale. These 15 prompts cover the full stack — from product copy to post-purchase emails to paid media strategy.

1. Product Copy & Listings

Prompt 1 — Product Page Copywriter

Write product page copy for my ecommerce store. Product: [name and description] Category: [what type of product] Target customer: [who buys this and why — be specific] Key features: [list 5–7] What makes it different from competitors: [your actual differentiators] Tone: [premium / playful / technical / lifestyle / minimal] Price point: $[price] Write: (1) Page headline (benefit-focused, under 10 words), (2) Sub-headline (expand on the promise, under 20 words), (3) Product description (150–200 words, tells a story before listing specs), (4) Feature bullet list (5 bullets — lead with the benefit, then the feature), (5) Who this is for / who this isn't for (optional but powerful), (6) FAQ section (5 objections as questions + answers). Write to convert, not just to describe.
Pro tip: Lead with who the product is FOR — "Built for runners who train in all weather" is more powerful than any list of features. Identity sells.

Prompt 2 — Collection Page Copy

Write collection page copy for [collection name: e.g., "Women's Running Gear" / "Gifts Under $50" / "Best Sellers"]. Store: [brand name] | Niche: [category] Collection purpose: [what this collection is for / the buying occasion] Audience: [who shops this collection] Key selling proposition of this collection: [what makes it worth browsing] Write: (1) Collection headline (under 8 words), (2) Collection description (75–100 words — speaks to the buyer's intent, not just the products), (3) 2 alternative versions (one more SEO-keyword focused, one more brand-voice focused). Also suggest: what filters or sorting options would help this audience find what they're looking for.

2. Email Marketing

Prompt 3 — Abandoned Cart Sequence

Write a 3-email abandoned cart recovery sequence for my ecommerce store. Store: [brand name] | Product(s) in cart: [general product type] Audience: [who shops here — describe] Brand tone: [friendly / direct / luxury / playful] Discount strategy: [no discount / 10% in email 3 / free shipping offer] Cart value: [typical abandoned cart value if known] Email 1 (1 hour after abandonment): Soft reminder — they left something behind. No discount yet. Email 2 (24 hours): Address the most likely objection. Still no discount. Email 3 (72 hours): Final recovery email with offer (if applicable). For each: Subject line + preview text + email body (150–200 words) + CTA. Tone: human and helpful, not desperate or pushy.
Pro tip: Email 1 typically recovers 30–50% of all recovered carts. Don't train buyers to wait for a discount by offering one too early in the sequence.

Prompt 4 — Post-Purchase Email Sequence

Write a 4-email post-purchase sequence for my ecommerce store. Store: [brand] | Product type: [what they bought] Average order value: $[amount] My goal: [repeat purchase / review / referral / upsell / all] Brand tone: [describe] Email 1 (order confirmation): More than just a receipt — build excitement. Email 2 (shipping / delivery day): Anticipation + quick tips for their first use. Email 3 (5–7 days post-delivery): Check-in + review request (honest, non-pushy). Email 4 (14–21 days): Value-add content + soft upsell or cross-sell. For each: Subject line → Preview text → Body (under 200 words) → CTA. These emails arrive when customer sentiment is highest — don't waste them on generic transactional copy.

Prompt 5 — Win-Back Campaign

Write a 3-email win-back campaign for customers who haven't purchased in [90 / 180 / 365] days. Store: [brand] | What they bought before: [product type] Average time between repeat purchases: [if known] Win-back offer (if any): [discount / free gift / early access / "no strings"] Email 1: "We miss you" — lead with value, not guilt. Remind them why they loved us. Email 2: New arrivals or improvements since they last shopped — show what's changed. Email 3: Last chance with offer (if applicable) + soft "is this goodbye?" moment. Subject lines + email bodies (150 words each) + CTAs. Flag anyone who doesn't respond for list suppression consideration.

3. Paid Advertising

Prompt 6 — Meta Ad Creative Pack

Write a Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad creative pack for my ecommerce store. Product: [name] | Price: $[price] Target audience: [demographics, interests, pain points] Campaign objective: [traffic / conversions / catalog sales] What I'm testing: [audience / creative / offer] Write 5 ad variations: For each — (1) Primary text (125 chars), (2) Headline (40 chars), (3) Description (30 chars), (4) Creative direction (what the image/video should show), (5) Psychological trigger used. Angles to cover across the 5 ads: (A) Problem-aware, (B) Outcome/aspiration, (C) Social proof/UGC style, (D) Urgency/scarcity, (E) Educational/"did you know." Don't repeat the same angle twice.
Pro tip: UGC-style creative (filmed like a customer's phone video) consistently beats polished studio ads in direct-response ecommerce. Test this format if you haven't.

Prompt 7 — Google Shopping Campaign Strategy

Build a Google Shopping and Performance Max strategy for my ecommerce store. Store: [brand] | Category: [product type] Monthly ad budget: $[amount] Top-selling products: [list 3–5] Current ROAS target: [number or "not sure"] Competitor price position: [cheapest / mid-market / premium] Strategy: (1) Campaign structure (Standard Shopping vs. PMax — which and why for my situation), (2) Asset groups and audience signals for PMax, (3) Bidding strategy and when to switch from manual to smart, (4) Product title optimization tips (Google weights this heavily), (5) Negative keyword strategy for Shopping. Calculate: what ROAS I need to be profitable given my margins.

4. Customer Experience & Reviews

Prompt 8 — Customer Service Response Templates

Write customer service response templates for my ecommerce store. Brand: [name] | Tone: [warm / professional / helpful] Product type: [general category] Templates for: (1) Order tracking inquiry (before delivery window), (2) Item arrived damaged, (3) Wrong item received, (4) "Where is my refund?", (5) Product doesn't match expectations, (6) Request to cancel before shipment, (7) Request for exchange, (8) Negative review on website (public response). Each template: Under 150 words, resolves the issue clearly, offers the right remedy, and makes the customer feel heard. Include a "make it right" option for each. Flag: which situations require manager review before responding.

Prompt 9 — Review Request Campaign

Write a review request strategy and templates for my ecommerce store. Platform: [Shopify product reviews / Google Reviews / Trustpilot / Judge.me / other] Average delivery time: [days after order] Brand tone: [warm / direct / playful] Incentive for reviews: [no incentive / loyalty points / discount on next order — check platform rules] Write: (1) Email subject line (non-manipulative — no "Leave us a 5-star review"), (2) Email body (under 150 words — lead with helping future customers, not our need for reviews), (3) SMS version (under 60 words), (4) How to respond to 1-star reviews vs. 5-star reviews, (5) Strategy for dealing with a sudden influx of negative reviews. Note: All review requests must comply with platform terms — never incentivize specific star ratings.

5. Conversion Optimization

Prompt 10 — Homepage Copy Audit

Audit and rewrite my ecommerce homepage copy. Current copy: [paste your current above-the-fold text, hero section, main sections] My brand: [name, niche, what we sell] Target visitor: [who lands on this page and what they're looking for] Current conversion rate: [if known] Top traffic source: [paid social / organic search / direct / etc.] Audit: (1) Is the value proposition clear in 5 seconds? (2) Does it speak to the visitor's goal or just describe us? (3) Is there a clear CTA above the fold? (4) What objections aren't being addressed? Then rewrite: Hero headline → Sub-headline → CTA button text → 3-section homepage structure. Make the visitor think "this is exactly for me" within seconds.
Pro tip: Your homepage hero has 5 seconds to answer "what is this, why should I care, what do I do next?" Most ecommerce homepages fail at all three.

Prompt 11 — A/B Test Idea Generator

Generate A/B test ideas to improve conversion rate for my ecommerce store. Store type: [Shopify / WooCommerce / etc.] | Product type: [what you sell] Current CVR: [%] | Average order value: $[amount] Traffic sources: [paid / organic / email — percentages if known] Pages with highest drop-off: [cart page / product page / checkout / landing page] Generate 10 high-value A/B tests ranked by: potential impact × ease of implementation. For each test: (1) What to test (hypothesis), (2) Control vs. treatment, (3) Which page, (4) Primary metric to measure, (5) How long to run it. Prioritize tests that address the biggest conversion barriers, not cosmetic tweaks.

6. Brand & Content

Prompt 12 — Brand Story & About Page

Write our brand story and About page copy. Brand name: [name] | What we sell: [product category] Founding story: [why you started — be specific, not generic] Who we're for: [our customer — describe them] What we believe: [brand values or mission] What makes us different: [real differentiators] Write: (1) Brand origin story (150 words — personal, specific, not corporate), (2) What we stand for (2–3 brand beliefs in plain language), (3) Who we're for (100 words — make your ideal customer feel seen), (4) Short version (50 words — for social bios and press). Avoid: "passion for," "dedicated to," "disrupting." Sound like a real person, not a startup pitch deck.

Prompt 13 — Content Marketing Plan

Build a content marketing plan for my ecommerce brand. Brand: [name] | Category: [products] | Target customer: [describe] Content goal: [SEO traffic / email list growth / brand awareness / all] Resources: [solo / small team, hours/week] Current content: [describe or "none"] Plan: (1) 3 content pillars (topics we can own that map to our audience's interests AND our product), (2) 10 blog post ideas with target keywords, (3) 5 email content ideas that don't feel like promotions, (4) Social content themes for [Instagram / Pinterest / TikTok — choose], (5) How to repurpose each blog post into 3+ pieces of content. Prioritize SEO content that drives purchase-intent traffic.

7. Strategy & Scaling

Prompt 14 — Holiday Campaign Strategy

Build a holiday campaign strategy for my ecommerce store. Store: [brand] | Revenue target this holiday: $[amount] Last year's holiday revenue: $[amount] (if applicable) My primary channel: [Meta Ads / email / organic / all] Timeline: [start date to end date] Budget for paid: $[amount] Discount strategy: [aggressive / moderate / no discounts — premium brand] Strategy: (1) Campaign calendar with key dates and focus for each phase (pre-Black Friday / BFCM / Cyber Week / last-minute / post-holiday), (2) Email sequence for the full holiday period (how many emails, themes, cadence), (3) Ad creative strategy by phase, (4) Inventory planning considerations, (5) The 1 thing that would make this holiday campaign stand out vs. every other brand shouting for attention.
Pro tip: Start your holiday email list-building 6–8 weeks early. A warm list in November is worth 3x more than cold audiences in December.

Prompt 15 — Annual Ecommerce Business Review

Help me review my ecommerce business this year. Revenue: $[amount] | Orders: [number] | AOV: $[amount] CVR: [%] | Email list size: [number] | ROAS: [number] Best-selling products: [list] Worst-performing products: [list] What worked: [marketing wins, product launches, optimizations] What didn't: [failures, wasted spend, operational issues] Where I am vs. goal: [honest gap] Analyze: (1) My strongest revenue lever — can I scale it? (2) What's my biggest acquisition cost problem? (3) The product or channel I should stop investing in, (4) Where I'm leaving money on the table (post-purchase, LTV, retention), (5) My #1 priority for Q1 and why it will have the most impact.
