From composition ideas to finding your style to selling your art — use AI as your creative partner and business coach without replacing what makes your work yours.

Creative Direction & Composition

1. The Composition Idea Generator

Staring at a blank canvas is the enemy of every artist. This prompt breaks the block fast.

I'm working on a [type of illustration/artwork] with the theme: [describe your theme or subject]. I use Procreate and my style leans toward [describe your style: loose/detailed, painterly/graphic, realistic/stylized]. Generate 5 different composition ideas I could explore: 1) For each, describe the focal point, the supporting elements, and how the eye moves through the piece, 2) Suggest a lighting approach for each (time of day, mood, direction), 3) Recommend which composition would be most eye-catching as a social media thumbnail, 4) Tell me which one would be most technically challenging and which would be most achievable. Give me something to actually put pencil to screen with.

2. The Color Palette Curator

Color can make or break a piece. This prompt gives you intentional palettes based on the mood you want to create.

I'm creating an illustration with this mood/vibe: [describe the feeling — e.g., cozy autumn evening, energetic summer, melancholy rainy day]. Generate 3 color palette options: 1) For each, name 5-6 specific colors using descriptive names (e.g., "dusty mauve," "burnt sienna," "sage green") plus approximate hex codes, 2) Designate which colors to use as: dominant (60%), secondary (30%), accent (10%), 3) Explain the psychological feeling each palette creates, 4) Suggest one "unexpected" color in each palette that would elevate the piece without clashing, 5) Recommend one palette for warm lighting and one for cool lighting.

3. The Subject & Scene Builder

I want to create a series of [number] illustrations around the theme of [broad theme]. Each piece should feel connected but stand alone. Help me design the series: 1) Propose [number] distinct scenes or subjects that fit the theme without being repetitive, 2) For each piece, suggest the main subject, the setting, the time of day, and one symbolic detail that adds depth, 3) Recommend a consistent visual element that ties the series together (color, shape, texture, character), 4) Suggest an order to post them that creates narrative tension — which comes first, which is the climax, which is the resolution.

Art Style Development

4. The Style Influence Analyzer

Understanding why you love certain artists helps you develop your own intentional style instead of accidentally copying.

I love these artists/styles: [list 3-5 artists or art movements you admire]. I work in Procreate doing [type of illustration]. Help me understand my aesthetic: 1) Identify the common visual elements across all my influences (line quality, color approach, composition style, subject matter), 2) Describe what a cohesive signature style might look like if I intentionally combined the best elements of these influences, 3) Suggest 2 specific exercises I could do in Procreate to develop that signature style, 4) Tell me what I should deliberately avoid so I'm not just a derivative of my influences, 5) Suggest one art movement or artist I haven't mentioned that I might connect with based on my taste.

5. The Consistent Style Prompt

I'm trying to create a consistent art style for my portfolio and social media. Right now, my work varies too much between pieces. My general vibe is: [describe your vibe]. Help me define a consistent visual language: 1) Recommend 3 "style rules" I should apply to every piece (e.g., "always use a limited palette of 4 colors," "always include one geometric element"), 2) Identify which Procreate brushes or brush types support this aesthetic, 3) Suggest a content niche I should focus on that fits this style (not just "illustration" — something specific like "soft botanical portraits" or "bold editorial figures"), 4) Write a 30-word style description I can put on my bio so people know what to expect.

6. The Creative Challenge Setter

I want to improve my Procreate skills and develop my style at the same time. Design a 30-day creative challenge for me. My current weak areas: [e.g., hands, dynamic poses, backgrounds, color harmony]. My style goal: [describe what you want your work to look like]. Create a challenge that: 1) Has a daily prompt for 30 days (specific enough to start drawing immediately), 2) Includes skill-focused days (practicing specific techniques) and expression days (just creating freely), 3) Builds progressively — early days should be achievable, later days should push me, 4) Includes 3-4 "consolidation" days where I apply what I've learned to a full piece. Make it feel like a curriculum, not a random list.

Growing Your Audience Online

7. The Instagram Caption Writer

Good captions turn viewers into followers. This prompt writes captions that work for art accounts.

I just posted a Procreate illustration of [describe the piece: subject, mood, style]. Write 5 different Instagram captions I could use: 1) A storytelling caption that describes the inspiration or emotion behind the piece, 2) A process caption that shares something about how I made it (technique, decision, struggle), 3) A question caption that invites genuine engagement (not "what do you think?"), 4) A short-and-punchy caption under 30 words, 5) A caption that positions me as an artist with a specific point of view. For each, suggest 10-15 relevant hashtags that would reach both art lovers and potential buyers.

8. The Content Calendar for Art Accounts

Create a 4-week Instagram content calendar for my Procreate art account. My niche: [describe your art style and subject matter]. My goals: [e.g., gain followers, sell prints, get commissions]. Post frequency: [X times per week]. Include a mix of: finished illustrations, work-in-progress videos, behind-the-scenes, tips or insights, and personal/process posts. For each post slot: 1) Describe what to post, 2) Suggest the format (static, reel, carousel), 3) Write a one-sentence hook for the caption, 4) Note whether it's audience-building or conversion-focused content.

9. The Speed Art Video Script

I want to make a Procreate speed art video for [platform: YouTube/Instagram Reels/TikTok]. The artwork is: [describe the piece]. Video length target: [X seconds/minutes]. Write a script/structure for the video: 1) Opening hook (first 3 seconds — what visual or text makes someone stop scrolling), 2) Pacing guide — which parts of the drawing should be shown at what speed, 3) Voiceover or text overlay commentary for key moments, 4) Background music mood recommendation, 5) Closing call-to-action. Also suggest one "twist" or unexpected element that makes the video more shareable than a standard timelapse.

Selling Your Art & Taking Commissions

10. The Commission Pricing Calculator

Most artists underprice commissions by not accounting for all the hidden time. This prompt helps you set rates that are actually profitable.

I take Procreate commissions for [type of art: portraits, character design, illustrations, etc.]. My skill level is [beginner/intermediate/advanced]. My approximate hours per piece: [X]. I want to make $[Y] per hour for my time. Help me build a commission price sheet: 1) Set prices for different complexity levels (simple, standard, complex), 2) Price add-ons: commercial rights, extra characters, rush fees, background complexity, 3) Write the policy text I should include (revisions, payment terms, what happens if they ghost me), 4) Suggest a pricing page layout that looks professional, 5) Tell me when I should raise my prices based on demand signals.

11. The Commission Form Writer

Write a commission inquiry form for my Procreate art business. I do [type of commissions]. I need a form that: 1) Collects everything I need before I can quote (type of piece, references, intended use, deadline, budget range), 2) Filters out clients who aren't serious or have unrealistic budgets, 3) Sets expectations about my process and timeline, 4) Has a "terms you agree to" section that protects me, 5) Sounds warm and human, not like a corporate intake form. Format it so I can use it as a Google Form or embed on my website.

12. The Print-on-Demand Product Idea Generator

I create Procreate illustrations in the style of [describe your style]. I want to start selling products on [Redbubble/Society6/Etsy/etc.]. Generate 10 product ideas that would work well with my illustration style: 1) Suggest specific products (not just "prints" — think sticker sheets, phone cases, journals, tote bags, etc.), 2) For each product, explain why my illustration style suits it, 3) Identify which products have the highest margin and search volume on print-on-demand platforms, 4) Suggest the ideal dimensions and format for each product, 5) Recommend which 3 products I should test first with my existing work.

Art Business & Income

13. The Niche Finder for Artists

Niche doesn't mean boring. It means being the go-to person for one specific thing — which is how artists build real audiences and income.

I'm a Procreate artist who currently creates [describe your current work broadly]. I'm trying to find a profitable niche so I'm not competing with every illustrator on the internet. My interests outside art include: [list 3-4 topics you genuinely care about]. My skills beyond illustration: [e.g., writing, design, teaching]. Help me: 1) Suggest 5 specific illustration niches at the intersection of my skills and interests, 2) For each niche, estimate the monetization potential (commissions, digital products, licensing, print-on-demand), 3) Identify which niche has the least competition relative to demand, 4) Describe the ideal first 10 pieces I would create to establish myself in that niche, 5) Tell me what to put in my bio to signal the niche immediately.

14. The Art Business Plan

I want to turn my Procreate art into a business that earns $[monthly goal]/month. Currently I earn $[current amount] from art. My main income streams so far: [list what you do: commissions, Etsy, etc.]. Time I can dedicate per week: [X hours]. Build a 90-day plan that: 1) Identifies my highest-leverage income stream to focus on first, 2) Sets weekly milestones that build toward the monthly goal, 3) Lists specific actions for each week (not vague goals — "post 3 reels" not "post more"), 4) Identifies what I need to stop doing to focus on what's working, 5) Tells me what the next phase looks like after I hit $[monthly goal].

15. The Art Licensing Intro Guide

I'm a Procreate artist interested in licensing my artwork for [product categories: e.g., stationery, apparel, home decor]. I've never done licensing before. Explain to me: 1) How art licensing works in simple terms (what I give, what I get, how contracts work), 2) The difference between exclusive and non-exclusive licensing and when each makes sense, 3) How to approach a company to pitch my work — what to send, how to find the right contact, what they're looking for, 4) How to price a licensing deal for a Procreate illustration, 5) The one thing I should never agree to in a licensing contract. Write this as if I'm completely new but not stupid.
Pro tip: The most powerful way to use these prompts is to combine them. Use Prompt 4 to define your style influences, then use Prompt 5 to build rules from that, then use Prompt 8 to plan content that shows off that style. AI works best when you're building a system, not just solving one-off problems.
